Google finally unveiled Nano Banana 2, the next version of its highly popular Nano Banana image model. This new model is based on the Gemini 3.1 Flash image model, so it’s fast and also 50% cheaper than the pro model.

Nano Banana 2 is now leading the text-to-image ranking on Arena AI.

Here are some of the key technical features:

-   **Real-Time world knowledge:** Integrated Google Search and Image grounding for accurate rendering of real-world landmarks and facts.
-   **Multi-subject consistency:** Industry-leading identity preservation for up to 4 characters and 10 objects across a sequence.
-   **Advanced typography:** Precise text rendering in multiple languages, perfect for professional infographics and global ad campaigns.
-   **Chain-of-thought reasoning:** Built-in “Thinking Level” configuration that analyzes complex prompts for superior logic and instruction following.
-   **4K ultra-HD & extreme ratios:** Native 4096×4096 resolution with support for specialized formats like 1:8, 8:1, 1:4, and 4:1.

Aside from the features above, Nano Banana 2 also supports up to 14 image inputs, generates up to 4K image outputs, and can handle complex spatial instructions.

**Now, let’s talk about where to access Nano Banana 2.**

Google has made the model accessible across their ecosystem. You can try it on Gemini, AI Studio, Vertex AI, Google Flow, and even on Google Ads.

Most of the regular users would use the Gemini app, but there’s one big catch. There are not many editing controls that you can toggle to customize the output. Take note that Nano Banana 2 supports more aspect ratios and multiple resolutions.
