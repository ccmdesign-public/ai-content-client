They say history doesn’t repeat itself, but it often rhymes.

When we look at the chaotic transformation of Rome from a decadent, inflation-ridden pagan state to a stabilized Christian empire, the parallels to our modern era are more than just striking.

They are a warning.

## Elagabalus vs. Wokeism

In the 220s AD, under the reign of the teenage emperor Elagabalus, the Roman Empire reached a breaking point of social and political absurdity. History shows us that the decay of institutions usually begins with the abandonment of proven traditions.

Elagabalus shattered Rome’s sacred pillars without hesitation: he attempted to marry a Vestal Virgin (a figure of sacred purity) and appointed a common barber to lead the empire’s grain supply, one of the most critical roles for the survival of the state.

Today, we are witnessing a similar radical deconstruction. Modern progressive ideologies target traditional hierarchies: biological sex is replaced by social constructs, and the classic family unit is being redefined through the elevation of alternative unions. Much like the communists of the past, today’s “woke” activists in the US and EU push for an artificial **“equality of outcome”** at the expense of the natural **“equality of opportunity.”**

This pressure for a “woke” agenda and the preferential treatment of select minorities creates a culture of sycophancy, just as it did in ancient Rome. **Meritocracy**, the rule of the capable, is being pushed into the shadows. It is being replaced by an anonymous bureaucracy managed by unelected officials who bear no real political responsibility, a parallel we see all too clearly in the structures in the US and Europe.

When Elagabalus paraded a conical black stone (the sun god Elagabal) through the streets, it was the ancient version of “virtue signaling.” Just as the elite were then forced to bow to a bizarre foreign cult to prove their loyalty to the regime, today’s radical political correctness acts as the new “black stone.” These ideologies prioritize “their truth” over the objective reality upon which civilization stands. The goal is the same: to enforce a version of social justice where a politically correct opinion matters more than competence, tradition, or the survival of the state.

## The “Chi-Rho” moment (Constantine/Trump parallel)

By 312 AD, the Roman Empire was fractured and failing.

Then came Constantine. He wasn’t a product of the Roman elites; he was a rugged commander from the frontier who, during the Battle of the Milvian Bridge, had a vision of a new sign: the **Chi-Rho**. This Christian monogram (the overlapping Greek letters X and P) was adopted as his military standard, symbolizing divine protection.

![Chi-Rho]()

Many modern observers see a direct parallel here with the moment Donald Trump experienced during the assassination attempt in Butler, Pennsylvania. Just as Constantine’s near-death experience led to the adoption of a “new sign” that defeated the old guard, the image of Trump with a bloodied ear is viewed by his supporters as a providential moment, a **“modern Chi-Rho.”** In both cases, this turning point transformed the leader from a mere politician into a figure with a higher mission: to replace a decaying establishment by returning to traditional values.

But Constantine was not just a religious reformer; he was a cold-blooded pragmatist. He understood that spirituality alone cannot save an empire if its financial infrastructure is in ruins.

## The Solidus vs. Bitcoin (The Hard Money argument)

Constantine’s greatest contribution wasn’t just the cross; it was the **Solidus**.

![Solidus]()

Rome was suffering from extreme inflation because previous emperors had regularly debased the silver content of the standard coin, the *denarius*, until it was essentially a worthless piece of scrap metal.

Constantine executed a radical reset. He confiscated the massive wealth stored in old pagan temples and used it to mint the Solidus, a coin of high-purity gold. It was a massive transfer of wealth from the old financial system to the new one.

In a modern context, this maneuver mirrors the aggressive shift toward crypto-friendly legislation and the push for a **“Strategic Bitcoin Reserve.”** Just as Constantine bypassed corrupt Roman mints, the proposal to integrate Bitcoin into the national balance sheet seeks to bypass the “denarius moment” of the Federal Reserve, the constant debasement of the dollar.

The goal is to replace centralized inflationary control with the transparent Bitcoin standard**.** If central banks are the “pagan temples” of our age, then the **Solidus of our time** is a digital gold that cannot be corrupted by political whim.

## The 1,000-year vision

We may be witnessing a calculated move inspired by the Roman past. The intent to link technological progress with a return to foundational values suggests a leadership that understands a civilization cannot survive on a debased currency and an emptied-out culture.

When Constantine introduced the Solidus and the Christian framework, he didn’t just provide a temporary band-aid. He laid the foundations for the Byzantine Empire, which preserved Western heritage and Roman law for another thousand years.

By adopting a “digital gold standard” and moving away from the inflationary “paganism” of modern central banking, this “Constantine Moment” could become the catalyst for stabilizing the West. If this modern reset succeeds, we may not be witnessing the fall of an empire, but the birth of a new, decentralized standard that secures our civilization for centuries to come.
