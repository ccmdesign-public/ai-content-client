**I switched from software engineering to data science eight months ago. Here’s what surprised me.**

![Software Engineer Turned Data Scientist: What Changed?]()

### 1\. Results Take Forever

As an engineer, I shipped features weekly. In data science, I spend weeks analyzing data just to find out there’s no useful pattern. It’s frustrating but normal.

### 2\. Code Quality Doesn’t Matter (Much)

I went from writing clean code to messy Jupyter notebooks with shortcuts everywhere. Data science is about experimenting fast, not building perfect systems.

![What Actually Changes When You Switch Careers]()

### 3\. You Talk More Than You Code

Half my time is spent in meetings explaining what models can’t do. You need to manage expectations and translate business problems into data questions. Communication skills matter more than I expected.

![How Data Scientists Actually Spend Their Time]()

### 4\. Career Growth is Unclear

Engineering has a clear path: junior to senior to principal. Data science roles are confusing—same title means different things at different companies. Senior positions are harder to find.

### 5\. You’ll Miss Building Real Products

Engineers build features users see and touch. Data scientists build models that run quietly in the background. The impact is less visible.
