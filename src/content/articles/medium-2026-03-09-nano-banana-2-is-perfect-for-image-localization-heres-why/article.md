Yes, Nano Banana 2 is revolutionary.

Much has already been said, tested, and praised, but there’s one more amazing use case that hasn’t been widely discussed.

Image localization.

With Nano Banana 2, adapting images to new markets becomes child’s play. Lightning-fast and unbelievably impressive.

Here are simple methods for quick image localization:

**1\. Replacing culturally sensitive objects**

Across cultures, some items are perceived differently. Some are taboo, some carry varied connotations. When running a marketing campaign, these are exactly the objects you need to consider.

Instead of organizing a new photo shoot for every target audience or browsing through the endless array of stock images, you can use one simple prompt. That’s it. The quickest way to perfectly adapt images for new audiences.

Here’s one example: a wellness campaign showing a woman relaxing with a glass of wine. A good approach for some countries, terrible for others. Luckily, you can convey a similar message by replacing the alcoholic beverage with something else. For example, a cup of tea.

![Screenshot by the Author]()

**2\. Replacing culturally sensitive colors**

This is a similar story to objects. Colors may carry different associations across cultures. Take black, for example. It’s generally perceived as suitable for luxury brands or professional settings, but it might be strongly associated with mourning in many African countries. Using it as a dominant brand color in this region without context can accidentally signal a somber atmosphere. These are the nuances every global brand should take into account.

Once you identify potential color misinterpretations, you can easily make your image market-ready with one simple prompt:

![Screenshot by the Author]()

**3\. Localizing text**

Sometimes the image is perfect, but the text is disruptive. Read: incomprehensible to your consumers across borders. Again, Nano Banana comes to the rescue. One quick prompt is all it takes to make the text as relevant as the image.

**4\. Localizing architectural context**

Every single detail matters. A perfectly fine object or person can look completely out of place if the background environment is wrong. Say you have an image shot in a Moroccan street that works great for your consumers in Morocco, but it won’t necessarily resonate with your Portuguese audience.

Nano Banana 2 can seamlessly teleport your model to a new location.

![Screenshot by the Author]()

Creating locally relevant images can be quick and easy if you mix these ingredients:

-   A pile of cultural knowledge
-   A huge amount of market research
-   And the key component: the right tool with the right prompts

Luckily, with tools such as Nano Banana 2, you’re covered when it comes to ingredient no. 3. All you need to do is add the remaining components, which is not a mission impossible either.
