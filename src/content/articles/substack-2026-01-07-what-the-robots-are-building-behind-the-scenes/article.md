# What the ROBOTS are building behind the scenes

**Welcome to today’s edition of ROBOTS ATE MY HOMEWORK. Today we’re stepping together into the director’s booth to storyboard a roadmap for 2026.**

Last time we chatted, we were freezing on the factory floor with 15+ AI creators, trying to break the blank slate panic of January. Today, the adrenaline has faded and the factory is a bit too quiet.

This blank slate we talked about at the Winter Ball in December felt like a massive threat. It was the silence before the AI started grinding again.

But I’m not standing here alone. I’ve helped a crew of little robots escape the “AI Circus” and they’ve worked for me ever since!

They have quirks, they have costumes, they’re trying to show off their personalities and they have very specific jobs. And they understand newsletters better than ever.

Here is exactly where we are taking the ROBOTS this year and what’s in it for you.

## 1\. The Great Migration from Substack Island

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!5rmg!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2f823e9e-449f-41e0-a107-07adac4651ee_1280x896.png)

My navigator robot Agamemnon is suspicious of the “Substack Islands.” He’s seen empires fall and platforms crumble, and he keeps pointing toward the horizon.

I love it here. I’ve made more genuine friends and found more “tribe” on Substack than I ever did on Twitter (rest in peace to the 2019 era) or LinkedIn.

The problem is a huge chunk of people have their email notifications turned off because they live inside the Substack app. That feels cozy right now, but what happens if Substack goes away, or if it shifts into a doom-and-gloom space that no longer feels like home? If I take my list and leave, but 50% of you only ever saw me because of an app notification you’ve since muted, do I really own that connection?

Agamemnon doesn’t like those odds. **So, in 2026, we are heading back out into the wild. I’m going to spend time promoting ROBOTS ATE MY HOMEWORK in the places where the walls don’t exist**: niche forums (yes, very 2000s, I know), private communities and deep-dive newsletter collaborations from outside of Substack.

I’ve seen [Jenny Ouyang](https://open.substack.com/users/282291554-jenny-ouyang?utm_source=mentions) building a dedicated Subreddit, [Karen Spinner](https://open.substack.com/users/363410124-karen-spinner?utm_source=mentions) has been playing more on LinkedIn, and [Anfernee](https://open.substack.com/users/154317088-anfernee?utm_source=mentions) is really great with Threads.

I want to know where you are spending your “thinking time” these days so I can steal your strategy too!

#### **❀ What you can learn from this:**

Don't let your business become a tenant on someone else's land. Use platforms for discovery, but build your own bridges to your audience via communities or forums so you aren't at the mercy of a single notification bell.

## 2\. Can a story be a sales strategy?

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!1qPt!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F0f35300c-b5b4-4c22-8616-4836cca08f50_1280x896.png)

When I first stepped onto the Substack floor, I did it to get more eyeballs on my consulting projects, and I definitely didn’t plan to “build a publication” (*initially, things definitely changed meanwhile).*

It’s always been a two-way street: I show people my skills, and in return, I show them the actual systems I can build for their own “factory.”

Up until now, I’ve been doing this in silence, and using Substack to do it on other platforms (LinkedIn). I haven’t plastered “Work With Me” banners everywhere or used this space as a traditional lead magnet. But my Harvey Specter robot thinks it’s time we stop being so modest about what we can do and become better closers.

**In 2026, I’m doubling down on this “Show, Don’t Tell” philosophy.**

Every workflow and process I share here is a prototype of the bespoke projects I build for founders and operators. I’m brainstorming ways to bring my consulting work more directly into the Substack ecosystem without losing the soul of the writing. There are so many brilliant creators here with unique voices, projects and personalities who are spending way too much time in the AI circus, so why not show them how they can build better systems.

I’ve been thinking a lot about the balance of this space too.

I love how [Neela 🌶️](https://open.substack.com/users/87891421-neela?utm_source=mentions), for example, uses Substack as her “home”, a place to share her life and thoughts rather than just a storefront for consulting. [JHong](https://open.substack.com/users/68897416-jhong?utm_source=mentions) is also doing a GREAT job bringing us into her life.

Harvey Specter-bot and I are looking for ways to maintain that human connection while making it very clear that if you want the source code for your own business, you know exactly who to call.

#### **❀ What you can learn from this:**

Your free content should be a “working demo” of your paid services. Show the gears of your process so clearly that the right clients realize they want BOTH the information and the person behind the system.

## 3\. Opening the curtains for a bigger cast

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!R6pk!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd43daf12-51c7-4386-9bdc-13b4dec87933_1280x896.png)

I just got back from a three-week holiday with no computers and cool places AND enough headspace to start day-dreaming.

Naturally, I came back with 20+ tasks and ideas that could fill a library. I realized that trying to cram everything into a once-a-week schedule was unsustainable.

**So, starting next week, we are doubling the output.**

**You’ll be seeing the ROBOTS in your inbox on Monday and Wednesday.**

The plan is two-fold:

1.  First, I want to show you even more specific systems and the mental models that fuel them, pushing the boundaries of how creative an AI can actually get when led by a human director.
    
2.  Second, this new schedule lets us tear down the walls for more collaborations. A once-a-week cadence put a hard limit on how many guests I could bring in. Now, the gates are open. Having that extra slot means I can reach out to the folks doing the most interesting work and bring their genius into our ecosystem.
    

If you’re building something high-taste and high-rigor, get ready cause I’m coming for you. And if you’ve got a system that needs a spotlight, don’t be shy and reach out.

My Wizard of Oz robot loves a good ensemble cast, and we finally have the stage space to let everyone shine.

#### **❀ What you can learn from this:**

Increasing frequency is cool if you keep the content as high quality as before. It’s also a great option if you want to have more “surface area” for partnerships. So, don’t be scared to increase your frequency but do it with the knowledge that you’ll have to keep the same high standards as before.

## 4\. Building your signature one brick at a time

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!DVer!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fb59462c9-6f70-4814-9d07-a633ecff6486_1280x896.png)

Next week I’m publishing a piece that acts as the absolute cornerstone for the 2026 branding of this publication.

It’s a deep dive into the system I used to re-position myself both inside the Substack ecosystem and in the wider world.

But I’m not doing this in a vacuum.

I’m building this together with YOU.

When that post drops, I’m inviting everyone to walk through that positioning workflow right alongside me. **I want everyone in this community to become a “Category of One” person. I’m here to help you find your unique strategic signature, no strings attached.**

Some people that have inspired me when it comes to branding (and visually, that is) are [AI Meets Girlboss](https://open.substack.com/users/415027717-ai-meets-girlboss?utm_source=mentions), [Jenny Ouyang](https://open.substack.com/users/282291554-jenny-ouyang?utm_source=mentions), [Dallas Payne](https://open.substack.com/users/324339337-dallas-payne?utm_source=mentions) and [Daria Cupareanu](https://open.substack.com/users/180057984-daria-cupareanu?utm_source=mentions).

Can’t speak on behalf of my Picasso robot though… he’s already looking at the canvas with a judgmental optical sensor.

#### **❀ What you can learn from this:**

A strong moat is built with better positioning, because if you sound like everyone else, you’re pretty much a commodity. You should invest in your visual identity as well as in your personality and quirks.

## 5\. Teaching the ROBOTS to speak HUMAN

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!QgYH!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F809a693d-ab21-4012-9ffa-2d8b7eeaf7a9_1280x896.png)

I’ve had beef with LinkedIn for years. If you’ve been following me for a while, you know I’ve tried probably ten different strategies to make that place work, and I never stick with them because I feel like it’s a minefield of cringe and performative hustle.

But in 2026, I’m calling a truce, mostly because I finally built a process that doesn’t make me want to throw my laptop out the window.

**I’m just going to extract ideas from this newsletter and post them three times a week.** I’m going to spend my actual energy engaging and meeting people. LinkedIn is my second-best source for leads, right after word-of-mouth, so it’s still worth being there.

I’m also going to stop trying to reinvent the wheel. I’ll be diving into [Melanie Goodman](https://open.substack.com/users/179266410-melanie-goodman?utm_source=mentions) ’s and [Ruben Hassid](https://open.substack.com/users/339636559-ruben-hassid?utm_source=mentions)’s strategies and actually putting them to good use. Plus, I have so many friends on there who are building incredible tools that I plan to shamelessly use to up my game ([Cedric](https://www.linkedin.com/in/cedricberrycroguennec/), if you’re reading this, I’m looking at you!).

Poor Glitch robot is pretty disappointed about this. He’d rather stay in the basement and organize my spreadsheets, but one of my big goals for 2026 is to make my robots actually love LinkedIn. Or at least tolerate it without short-circuiting.

#### **❀ What you can learn from this:**

Systematize your distribution so you can humanize your engagement. Use your long-form content as a “mine” for social posts so you spend less time performing for the bots and more time talking to humans.

## 6\. Is the strongest team the one you married?

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!G2Fe!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdb9cfafd-3b0a-4b55-8a0e-576617ccb0d6_1280x896.png)

You might not know this, but my husband, [Dan Cucolea](https://open.substack.com/users/44242110-dan-cucolea?utm_source=mentions) has been a dedicated Substack lurker for a while now. He recently finally made the jump and started his own newsletter, and I couldn’t be more excited.

He’s (and I say this with zero bias, truly) the best product builder I know. He’s spent years consulting for 8-figure companies on conversion optimization and product strategy, not to mention leading our content agency and our own product builds.

Together, we’ve realized we’re a bit of a power couple in this space. I know exactly where my skills shine (and I’m not afraid to brag about them), but I also know exactly where I fall short. I’m grateful to have him as the structural backbone for our more technical builds.

**In 2026, we’re going all in on building even more products together. We want to take the systems and strategies I talk about here and turn them into tangible tools and products.**

We don’t need any new robots for this section. The army we already have is more than enough to handle the workload. Plus, I’ve got a lot to learn from people like [Karo (Product with Attitude)](https://open.substack.com/users/27968736-karo-product-with-attitude?utm_source=mentions) and [Elena Calvillo at Product](https://open.substack.com/users/31598723-elena-calvillo-at-product?utm_source=mentions).

#### **❀ What you can learn from this:**

Audit your own “structural backbone.” Know where your skills end and where you need a partner or a system to take over. Also, I’d advise you to work with your spouse, but I know this doesn’t work for everyone and marriages can break. Luckily, it’s been the opposite for us!

## 7\. A newsletter as an operating system

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gDsl!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc2cc10d5-a48a-4970-bb44-138a27ad9955_1280x896.png)

**I want to make a promise right now: all my articles and workflows will always be free.**

I’ve been thinking a lot about how to monetize this newsletter, and it won’t be through the content itself.

I’m a content creator at heart, and gating my work just doesn’t sit right with me. I’m a total binge reader, and I want you to be one, too. Binge reading is how we build real trust.

Plus, every workflow I share is a starting point, it will never work the same for you as it does for me, so charging for a “template” feels unfair.

My goal is to transform ROBOTS ATE MY HOMEWORK into a full-scale media company, and I don’t think I’d personally be able to do that by gating.

So, what’s left?

If the content is free, how do the lights stay on?

Well, as I mentioned previously, I’m in the business of bringing products to life. I have a powerhouse team that helps builders “product better,” both in their businesses and for themselves. Instead of charging for reading, I’ll be building a paid tier that gives you access to something tangible, like a custom-trained AI tool, fine-tuned by the very systems I share here.

It’s going to be a Perplexity-style engine, but trained specifically on the ROBOTS ATE MY HOMEWORK philosophy and your own unique brand. Dan is helping me bring this vision to life, and I couldn’t be more excited to share the messy, brilliant process of building it with you.

I’ll be showing you everything, how we’re architecting the tech, how I’m positioning it in a crowded market, and how I’m training the AI to think like an architect.

It’s going to be an adventure, and I’m planning plenty of other cool perks for the paid community as we go. But we’ll take it one step at a time - when the time comes!

The library doors will always be wide open, and for now, the secret lab is officially under construction.

#### **❀ What you can learn from this:**

Gating information is cool and it works for the majority of newsletters. Try to audit your approach and see if it works for you because gating content is NOT the only way to monetize. A lot of cool kids keep the library open to build trust but monetize the engine (the specialized tools and implementation that saves people time).

## 8\. The art of the micro pivot

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!J8kA!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9a13fbe5-82b6-4b5a-8c99-6c7d4bda6a84_1280x896.png)

The Scribe robot knows a secret that the AI circus hasn’t figured out yet: anything that doesn’t iterate is just a paperweight.

Every single newsletter I send is, in its own small way, a rebrand. I pivot every single day.

**BUT (a big but) I pivot within the boundaries of my niche and my core philosophy. Most of you don’t even notice the big shifts. To the outside world, I just start sounding more and more like exactly what you need.**

I really want to drive home the idea that it’s okay to pivot.

I learned this from watching folks like [Mack Collier](https://open.substack.com/users/13158830-mack-collier?utm_source=mentions), [James Presbitero](https://open.substack.com/users/112381382-james-presbitero?utm_source=mentions) and [Tracy Friedlander](https://open.substack.com/users/4878855-tracy-friedlander?utm_source=mentions). They showed me that pivots are the only way we actually grow. If you aren’t evolving, you’re just becoming a commodity. The “Commodity Trap” is full of people who found a formula in 2022 and are still trying to force it to work today.

There’s a quieter side to pivoting that doesn’t mean changing your entire identity. It’s the little things, the way you frame a problem or the specific cultural hooks you use to anchor your strategy. These micro-adjustments are how you stay aligned with your audience as they grow alongside you.

#### **❀ What you can learn from this:**

Don’t be afraid to pivot - big or small. It doesn’t have to be a total rebrand. It can be a subtle shift in tone or framing.

## 9\. Why build frameworks when you could build worlds?

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!GIPQ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F8769a18a-a78a-404a-9760-f9eb85290afc_1280x896.png)

Orson Robotwells, my tiny director-robot in a beret, is staging a full-on coup in 2026. He’s decided the days of “here’s a clean list of prompts” are over. Everything needs a plot, a character, a little cinematic chaos layered on top of the system.

And honestly, he’s right.

**I’m planning to bring even more storytelling, characters, and ongoing plots into my AI systems this year for a few reasons.**

1.  First, stories get attention in a way frameworks alone never will. If I wrap a workflow inside a robot heist or a factory-floor drama, your brain actually wants to stick around and see what happens next, which means you’re way more likely to follow the steps and use the system.
    
2.  Second, it’s just more fun, for you and for me. If I’m bored writing it, you’ll be bored reading it.
    
3.  Third, I’ve always believed the real edge comes from mixing your personal quirks with your “professional” brain. When I bake my obsessions (film, characters, books, culture) into these AI workflows, besides getting the instructions you also get a part of *me*, and that builds trust. That’s how you piss off the robots: you give them a soul they can’t copy.
    
4.  And finally, I didn’t go to film school and build a whole film background for nothing, did I? If 2026 isn’t the year I fully merge cinematic storytelling with hardcore AI systems, then what was all that training even for?
    

#### **❀ What you can learn from this:**

Logic makes people think but stories make them act. If you want people to actually use your systems, wrap them in a narrative that makes the technical details feel like a part of a larger adventure. People will DREAM about these at night and trust me, that’s how you have them coming back.

## 10\. More seats at the Christmas table

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gEE_!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fe8d76701-c02a-4448-b598-69b76bb3d413_1280x896.png)

I’ve made so many incredible friends in this space lately, and it’s reminded me that connection is the true core of building anything that lasts.

I learned a lot from [Sam Illingworth](https://open.substack.com/users/253722705-sam-illingworth?utm_source=mentions) about this and how the foundation of any “Category of One” brand isn’t just the systems you build, but also the tribe you keep. In 2026, I’m moving away from relying on generic “explore” pages or waiting for an algorithm to introduce me to my next collaborator.

**I want to grow my circle, but I want to do it with intention.**

The current discovery systems on platforms like Substack often feel like a game of telephone with a broken wire. They don’t always surface the people I’m looking for.

So, I’m taking the manual approach. I want to find more ways to collaborate, speak to more people and genuinely lift others up as I grow. My current circle is already LEGENDARY, but there is always room for more brilliant minds at the table.

If I can use my platform to shine a light on other creators while we swap systems and strategies, everyone wins.

The Sparky bot is already warming up his communication modules cause 2026 is going to be the year of the human connection in an increasingly bot-filled world.

#### **❀ What you can learn from this:**

Don’t wait for an algorithm to find your peers, go out and manually curate your circle. A “Category of One” is stronger when it’s part of a legendary tribe.

## 11\. What happens when the director finally faces the camera?

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!KYc-!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F90ae1b76-e067-4810-b6c8-fd8dbe5b51c6_1280x896.png)

I’ve been getting pulled into the “come do a live with me” universe. People I love and appreciate like [Sam Illingworth](https://open.substack.com/users/253722705-sam-illingworth?utm_source=mentions), [Juan Salas-Romer](https://open.substack.com/users/71734539-juan-salas-romer?utm_source=mentions) and [Phil Powis ❤️⚡️](https://open.substack.com/users/181219008-phil-powis?utm_source=mentions) have already invited me, and I’m seeing more and more creators experiment with video.

**You’d think that someone with a film background would be dying to jump on camera, but plot twist: I am absolutely not that person.**

That’s the funny part. I’ve done a lot of theatre and film, both as a director and as a character, so I *know* I have the skill set for this. It’s not a confidence issue (*who am I lying?)* as much as a bandwidth and timing one; I already have a lot of projects under my belt, and the idea of adding video on top sometimes feels like inviting chaos into an already loud factory.

That said, I’m pretty sure 2026 is the year I at least dip my toes into lives or some kind of video format. I just need to brainstorm it to the moon and back until I find a format that feels like *me*, something that mixes systems, storytelling, and a bit of theatre without turning into a generic talking-head stream.

Plus, I really don’t want to disappoint [Phil Powis ❤️⚡️](https://open.substack.com/users/181219008-phil-powis?utm_source=mentions). He already said that part of why he appreciates me is that I keep refusing…

#### **❀ What you can learn from this:**

Having the skill for something doesn’t mean you have to do it the “standard” way. If you’re going to step into a new format like video, wait until you find a way to do it that honors your unique background.
