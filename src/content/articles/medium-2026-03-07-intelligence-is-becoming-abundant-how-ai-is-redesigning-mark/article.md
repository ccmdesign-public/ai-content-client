# Intelligence Is Becoming Abundant: How AI Is Redesigning Markets, Pricing, and Risk

# Intelligence Is Becoming Abundant: How AI Is Redesigning Markets, Pricing, and Risk

[RAKTIM SINGH](/@raktims2210?source=post_page---byline--171191bf1154---------------------------------------)

5 min read·Feb 25, 2026

\--

![]()

For most of economic history, intelligence was scarce.

Judgment was limited by human bandwidth.
Analysis was expensive.
Coordination required hierarchy.
Strategy moved at the speed of meetings.

That constraint is collapsing.

Artificial intelligence is driving the marginal cost of applied cognition toward zero.

Reasoning, summarization, pattern recognition, forecasting, negotiation support, and evaluation are no longer rare executive functions. They are programmable infrastructure.

And when a major input cost collapses, markets reorganize.

**This Is Not “Better Software.” It Is a Cost Collapse.**

Industrialization reduced the cost of physical energy.
The internet reduced the cost of information transmission and discovery.
AI is reducing the cost of applied cognition.

Cognitive cost means the cost of producing decision-useful intelligence — fast enough, cheap enough, and repeatable enough to be applied everywhere.

When intelligence is expensive, you apply it periodically.

When intelligence becomes cheap, you apply it continuously.

And that is when industries recompose.

![]()

## **The Law of Cognitive Cost Collapse**

When the marginal cost of applied cognition falls sharply, markets move from periodic, human-mediated coordination to continuous, algorithmic coordination — and industries reorganize around whoever controls the new intelligence loops.

This triggers predictable shifts:

1.  Pricing becomes dynamic
2.  Risk becomes continuous
3.  Negotiation becomes algorithmic
4.  Matching becomes granular
5.  Trust and proof become products

This is the economic foundation of what I call the **Third-Order AI Economy** — where AI does not just optimize workflows but reshapes market architecture.

## **Why This Matters Now**

Evidence suggests that productivity gains from AI are uneven and still emerging. Many firms report limited measurable improvements so far.

That is not surprising.

Because the early phase of technological cost collapse rarely shows up first as productivity.

It shows up as pressure.

Value migration begins before value creation becomes obvious.

Margins compress.
Intermediation weakens.
Profit pools shift.

The real question is not:

“Are we using AI?”

The real question is:

“Are we redesigning pricing, risk, negotiation, and trust around continuous intelligence?”

![]()

## **A Practical Lens: The C.O.R.E. Market Loop**

To operationalize this shift, I describe a simple loop:

C — Comprehend context
O — Optimize decisions
R — Realize action
E — Evolve through evidence

This turns cheap cognition into continuous market advantage.

This is not just an enterprise model.

It is a market model.

## **Why This Is Structural, Not Cyclical**

Coase showed that firms exist because transaction costs are high.

Hayek showed that knowledge is dispersed.

Simon showed that rationality is bounded.

AI does not eliminate those truths.

But it reduces the cognitive component of transaction costs.

That changes firm boundaries.
It changes coordination speed.
It changes negotiation economics.

And it shifts advantage toward institutions that design safe, governed intelligence loops — not just powerful models.

![]()

## **The Board-Level Imperative**

Boards should ask:

-   Where is pricing still periodic in a continuous world?
-   Where does uncertainty create margin that AI will compress?
-   Which negotiations are expensive only because cognition is expensive?
-   What proof systems will differentiate us when intelligence becomes abundant?

The AI story is not about replacing humans.

It is about redesigning markets around better outcomes.

The winners will not be the firms running the most pilots.

They will be the institutions that understand this as a structural economic event — and act accordingly.
