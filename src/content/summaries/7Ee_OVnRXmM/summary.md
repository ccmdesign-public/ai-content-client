## Key Takeaways

The 2026 State of AI report reveals significant shifts in tool adoption and hardware demands. • **Quen** has surpassed Meta Lama as the most deployed open-source LLM for **self-hosted setups**. • **Comfy UI** now powers nearly 70% of all image workflows, with a focus on upscaling over generation. • The industry is adopting **H200 and V200 GPUs** to handle complex AI pipelines as hardware demand surges.

## Summary

Runpod's 2026 State of AI report provides a data-driven snapshot of how developers are currently building with AI, revealing major shifts in model popularity, workflow focus, and infrastructure needs.

In the large language model space, **Quen** has emerged as the new leader for self-hosted deployments, overtaking the previously dominant **Meta Lama**. This indicates a changing preference in the open-source LLM ecosystem for production setups.

For image generation, **Stable Diffusion** still powers 67% of image endpoints, but faces strong competition from the rapidly growing **Flux** model. Interestingly, the data shows developers are spending more time **upscaling images** rather than generating new ones from scratch.

Nearly 70% of all image workflows now rely on a single tool: **Comfy UI**. This highlights the consolidation happening in the AI tooling space as specific solutions gain dominant market share for particular tasks.

In video generation, a model called **WAN** is currently dominating the scene, while audio workflows remain primarily consumption-focused. **Transcription** is six times more common than text-to-speech, with **OpenAI's Whisper** model leading this category for developers.

All this advanced AI generation is creating massive hardware demands. To keep up with increasingly complex pipelines, the industry is shifting toward **H200 and V200 GPUs** to handle the sheer scale of work. The good news is that pricing has trended downward across the board over the last year as supply finally starts catching up with demand.

## Context

This report matters because it provides concrete data on actual AI adoption patterns rather than hype or speculation. Developers, AI engineers, and infrastructure teams need to understand these shifts to make informed decisions about which models to adopt, which tools to invest in learning, and what hardware to plan for. The findings reveal consolidation trends in the AI tooling ecosystem and highlight where the industry is allocating resources as AI moves from experimentation to production deployment.
