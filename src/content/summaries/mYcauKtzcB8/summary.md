## Key Takeaways

The video compares three leading AI models' web design capabilities through practical testing. • **GPT-5.4's design skill** produces professional but highly consistent designs across different prompts • **Claude Opus with front-end skill** creates more unique designs by avoiding common fonts and color schemes • **Gemini 3.1 Pro** delivers clean, straightforward designs that are often the most practical for real websites • **Design skills significantly impact output**, with strict guidelines in Codeex versus creative freedom in Cloud Code

## Summary

The video presents a head-to-head comparison of GPT-5.4, Claude Opus, and Gemini 3.1 Pro for generating landing page designs. The creator tested four different design prompts across all three models, including building a fictional AI startup landing page inspired by three popular designs (SuperX, Notion, Slack) and redesigning their own AI Coding Daily website.

**GPT-5.4 with Codeex design skill** consistently produced professional-looking dark-themed designs with specific structural patterns. All outputs followed a strict sequence: headline first, body second, CTA fourth. The designs were visually appealing but showed remarkable similarity across different prompts, suggesting the skill enforces rigid design rules. The menu behavior also differed

- Codeex designs made the navigation disappear on scroll, while other models kept it fixed.

**Claude Opus with Cloud Code front-end skill** generated more unique designs with distinctive font choices and color schemes. The skill specifically instructs avoiding generic, overused fonts and clichéd color schemes (like purple gradients), pushing for more creative solutions. However, the creator noted some font choices felt unconventional and potentially less readable. When tested without the front-end skill, Opus produced more Bootstrap-like, generic designs reminiscent of 2010s web aesthetics.

**Gemini 3.1 Pro** delivered clean, simple designs with white backgrounds and straightforward layouts. For the website redesign task specifically, Gemini produced the creator's favorite result

- a practical, clean design that emphasized subscription elements and premium content visibility. Gemini's designs were less flashy but more immediately usable for real websites.

The video also analyzed the underlying skill structures. **Codeex's design skill** enforces strict rules about layout sequences and component usage. **Cloud Code's front-end skill** focuses on avoiding predictability and creating 'unforgettable' designs through unique font and color choices. This fundamental difference explains why Codeex produces consistent, professional results while Cloud Code aims for more distinctive, creative outputs.

For practical applications, the creator suggests: GPT-5.4 for reliable professional designs, Claude Opus with front-end skill for unique creative projects, and Gemini for clean, functional website redesigns where usability trumps uniqueness.
