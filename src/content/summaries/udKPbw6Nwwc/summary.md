## Key Takeaways

Ahrefs' August 2025 update focuses on expanding brand monitoring into AI-powered search and social media while improving core SEO tools. Key updates include: • **Brand Radar 2.0** now tracks brand mentions in AI answers (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI) and includes new export features. • **Social Media Management** beta allows planning and scheduling posts across LinkedIn, X, Instagram, and Facebook directly in Ahrefs. • **YouTube backlinks** are now discoverable thanks to crawler improvements, and the **AI detection score** has been refined for more accurate content analysis.

## Summary

The August 2025 Ahrefs product update delivers 19 significant enhancements, headlined by the major evolution of **Brand Radar** to version 2.0. The tool has been restructured to mirror Site Explorer's layout and now provides a comprehensive view of a brand's online footprint across AI-generated answers on platforms like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google's AI overviews. New reports—**Cited Domains** and **Cited Pages**—show how often tracked brands are mentioned in AI responses with backlinks. Google AI mode tracking, now live in the US, UK, and India, is bundled with AI Overviews for $199/month and included in the All Platforms add-on at no extra cost. The update also adds long-requested export functionality and proper limits.

A completely new **Social Media Management** feature enters beta, enabling users to plan, schedule, and manage posts across LinkedIn, X, Instagram, and Facebook directly within the Ahrefs platform. This integration aims to save time by consolidating content management. The feature is free during its beta period.

**Site Explorer** receives several impactful updates. The platform can now pick up **YouTube backlinks** due to crawler improvements, useful for link-building and brand monitoring on the video platform. The **Ads report** has been rebuilt with new paid search logic that checks older SERPs to fill data gaps, providing a clearer picture of paid search visibility over time. The **AI detection score** has been significantly improved: it now distinguishes between pure AI-generated content and mixed human/AI content, counts mixed content lower, and reduces false positives for a more accurate assessment. This detection is also now available in the Site Audit's URL details report.

Other notable improvements include: **Web Analytics** now allows saving filter presets per user and project, and comparison mode adds change columns across all reports. **GSC reports** gain a visibility trend chart from Site Explorer data and URL filtering in overviews. **Rank Tracker** introduces project-level keyword limits for enterprise scale control and adds a search volume chart to the performance report. Finally, the **API** receives a new Site Audit endpoint that returns a list of issues for any project, with options to compare changes between crawls.

## Context

This update reflects Ahrefs' strategic expansion beyond traditional SEO into the broader digital marketing and brand management landscape. As AI-powered search (like Google's AI Overviews) and social media become increasingly critical for brand visibility and audience engagement, SEO professionals and marketers need integrated tools to monitor and manage their presence across these fragmented channels. These updates position Ahrefs as a more holistic platform for tracking brand mentions in AI-generated content, managing social outreach, and obtaining a more accurate, comprehensive view of online performance.
