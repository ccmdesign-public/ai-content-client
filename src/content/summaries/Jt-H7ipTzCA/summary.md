## Key Takeaways

Proof transformed from a local tool for content attribution to a collaborative platform for human-AI teamwork. The key insights are:

• **Origin as a content attribution tool**: Started as a Mac app with a gutter showing purple for AI-written and green for human-written content.
• **Pivot to collaborative workspace**: The killer feature became sharing documents where humans and agents can work together simultaneously.
• **Live human-agent collaboration**: Evolved into a web-based document where both humans and AI agents can make changes, leave comments, and track changes in real-time.

## Summary

Proof began with a simple but important premise: distinguishing between human-written and AI-generated content while understanding the thought process behind each piece. The original version was a Mac application featuring a visual gutter system that used purple to indicate AI content and green for human content—a feature that remains in the product today.

During development, the creator built an impressive Mac application with sophisticated internal AI agent integration, including sub-agents and advanced functionality. However, in practical use, a different value proposition emerged as the most compelling aspect.

The real breakthrough came from recognizing that the most valuable feature wasn't the local analysis capabilities, but rather the ability to share and collaborate on documents. This realization emerged from real-world scenarios where someone working with an AI agent on a project needed to share the resulting plan with colleagues like Kieran.

Proof evolved into a **live collaborative web-based document** that serves as a shared workspace where both humans and AI agents can participate simultaneously. This platform enables real-time collaboration where humans and their AI counterparts can make changes, leave comments, use track changes functionality, and work together on content creation and refinement.

The transition from a local analysis tool to a collaborative platform represents a fundamental shift in how humans and AI agents can work together effectively in shared digital spaces.

## Context

This video matters because it addresses a critical challenge in the AI era: establishing provenance and collaboration between human and AI-generated content. As AI writing tools become ubiquitous, organizations and individuals need ways to track authorship, maintain accountability, and enable productive human-AI teamwork. The evolution of Proof from a simple attribution tool to a collaborative platform reflects the growing need for shared workspaces where humans and AI agents can work together transparently and effectively.
