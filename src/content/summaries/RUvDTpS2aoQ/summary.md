## Key Takeaways

Claude now offers a free, streamlined process to import your personal AI memory from other platforms. • **Free memory import**: Previously a paid feature, memory is now available to all users at no cost. • **Simple transfer process**: Use the prompt from claude.com/import-memory in your old AI to generate a summary for Claude. • **Strategic user retention**: This move capitalizes on recent growth, aiming to lock in new users by making Claude feel personalized immediately.

## Summary

Anthropic has removed the paywall for Claude's memory feature, making it accessible to every user. This allows you to transfer your entire personal context—including your name, work preferences, and details of ongoing projects—from other AI assistants like ChatGPT or Gemini into Claude in seconds.

The process is straightforward. First, visit the dedicated page at **claude.com/import-memory** and copy the provided prompt. Next, paste this prompt into the AI you've been using (e.g., ChatGPT). That AI will then generate a summary of your memory and preferences. Finally, you paste this summary directly into Claude, and the transfer is complete.

This development is particularly significant given the recent surge in Claude's popularity. Following a major news event involving the US government, Claude experienced a massive wave of new signups, reaching number one in the app store. The free memory import is a strategic tool for **user retention**, designed to make new users feel understood from their very first interaction, thereby increasing the likelihood they will continue using the service.

Previously, users could attempt a manual transfer by asking their old AI to export information, but this was cumbersome and required knowing the right prompts. The new tool simplifies this into a single, guided workflow, lowering the barrier for users to switch to Claude and bring their established AI history with them.

## Context

This announcement is a direct competitive move in the rapidly evolving AI assistant market. Following a significant news event that drove a surge of new users to Claude, Anthropic is making a key feature free to reduce friction for switching from platforms like ChatGPT. This matters to anyone using AI for work, as it allows seamless migration of personal context, making the new assistant immediately useful. It highlights the industry's focus on user experience and retention as a key battleground beyond raw model capabilities.
