## Key Takeaways

This video provides a framework for hiring and compensating key leadership talent to scale a business while maintaining owner control and margins.

- **Hire an operator, not a C-suite executive:** At a $5.5M revenue level, you need a VP/Director or GM who is a people leader, not just a systems/process person.

- **Use targeted outreach for recruitment:** LinkedIn outreach to 500+ candidates in similar roles/industries is more effective than job boards like Indeed for this level.

- **Structure compensation with profit share and phantom equity:** Create a 10-20% profit pool for leaders and use phantom equity (e.g., 1% per year vesting) to give cash flow and exit upside without giving away risk or control.

- **Reverse engineer compensation based on target earnings:** Determine the total compensation (OTE) the role should command, then back into the profit share percentage needed to hit that number.

## Summary

The video features a business coaching session with an auto hail repair shop owner doing $5.5 million in revenue across four locations, who aims to scale to $15 million. His primary constraint is people leadership, as all operational decisions funnel back to him.

### Identifying the Right Hire

The coach immediately corrects the owner's framing: at this revenue level, he doesn't need a C-suite executive (COO/CMO). He needs a high-caliber **operator**—a VP, Director, or General Manager. This person must be a **leader who manages people**, not just someone obsessed with processes and systems. The ideal candidate is someone the existing team will admire and go to with problems, effectively decentralizing operations from the owner.

### Recruitment Strategy

For this senior level, **targeted outreach on LinkedIn** is recommended over generic job ads. The owner should identify 500+ people currently in similar operational roles at auto repair businesses of comparable size and pitch them directly. If local talent is scarce, offering a **relocation package or signing bonus** can expand the candidate pool.

### Compensation Structure to Scale and Retain Control

The core of the advice focuses on designing compensation that scales performance without eroding margins or owner control.

- **Profit Share Pools:** Create a pool of 10-20% of the business's overall profits to distribute among leaders. This aligns their incentives with profitability and growth, as the pie increases for everyone. This can be applied at both the overall business level for a top operator and at the individual shop level for location managers.

- **Phantom Equity / Profits Interest:** Instead of giving away real equity (which comes with risk and control), use a **phantom equity** structure. For example, offer a 4% profits interest that vests at 1% per year. This grants a right to a percentage of the sale proceeds only upon a liquidity event, and it returns to the company if the employee leaves. This provides the cash flow and exit upside employees want without the owner relinquishing control or the employee taking on real risk.

- **Reverse Engineering OTE:** Don't fixate on a specific percentage (e.g., 5% vs. 10%). First, decide the **On-Target Earnings (OTE)** the role deserves (e.g., $150,000). Then, work backwards: if a shop makes $50,000 monthly profit, a 10% share yields $5,000/month ($60k/year) in bonus. Combine that with a base salary to hit the target OTE. This ensures you attract the right caliber of person.
