## Key Takeaways

Based on LinkedIn's labor market data, the future of work requires a strategic blend of technical and human skills. Key takeaways: • **AI is a net job creator**, adding 1.3M new roles like **data annotators**, not a cause of current hiring slowdowns. • Future-proof careers require **AI literacy** and mastering **human skills** (curiosity, creativity, courage, compassion, communication). • **Career paths are no longer linear**; focus on acquiring new skills every few months over rigid 5-year plans. • High-demand jobs include **data annotators**, **data center infrastructure roles**, and **forward-deployed engineers** who bridge AI and business needs.

## Summary

Ryan Roslansky, CEO of LinkedIn and executive vice president at Microsoft, discusses the evolving job market through the lens of LinkedIn's data, which he describes as the definitive labor market platform. He clarifies that while global hiring is sluggish, this is due to macroeconomic factors like interest rates, not AI. In fact, AI has been a net positive, creating 1.3 million new jobs, including roles like data annotators and data center jobs.

A major shift is the move away from linear career paths. Roslansky notes that the skills required for a given role have changed over 25% in recent years and are projected to change 70% by 2030, largely driven by AI. Therefore, professionals should focus less on where they want to be in five years and more on **what new skills to learn in the next few months**. The most successful individuals will be **generalists** who expand their skill sets horizontally.

The essential skills for the future are a dual focus: **AI literacy** and core **human skills**. He emphasizes five critical 'C's: **curiosity, creativity, courage, compassion, and communication**. These 'human skills' are not soft but essential for standing out, as AI excels at tasks like summarization and translation, making uniquely human capabilities more valuable.

Roslansky identifies three high-growth job categories for the next 3-5 years:

- **Data Annotators**: Experts who evaluate and improve AI model outputs in their field of expertise (e.g., a cardiologist reviewing medical AI responses).

- **Data Center Jobs**: All roles involved in building and maintaining the physical and technical infrastructure required for AI, from trades to hyper-technical positions.

- **Forward-Deployed Engineers**: Professionals who sit within business units (like marketing or product) to translate business needs into practical AI applications, ensuring AI delivers real business value.

He also highlights the rise of the **creator economy**, with 4 million LinkedIn members listing 'creator' as their full-time job. For career growth, he advises using LinkedIn to **demonstrate expertise through content**, as recruiters increasingly look at posts and skills over traditional credentials like university names. The value of a college degree is shifting more towards developing social and human skills rather than just hard technical knowledge.
