## Summary

The video discusses a critical security crisis emerging from the rapid adoption of AI agents in enterprises.

A recent study found that **91% of enterprise customers are aware they have AI agents deployed in production**. However, when asked if they were confident these agents were appropriately secured, the number plummeted to just **10%**. This stark disparity highlights a massive governance gap.

Furthermore, **88% of companies experienced an AI agent security incident this year**, and most were unaware it was happening. These incidents can be severe, including agents deleting files or transferring money autonomously.

### The Nature of Agentic Identities

The problem stems from the fundamental nature of **agentic identities**. Unlike human or service accounts, AI agents can operate autonomously and may possess privileges that administrators are not fully aware of. The current infrastructure relies on **app-to-app connections**, but the future requires **app-to-agent or agent-to-agent connections**. This necessitates creating and managing a new type of identity.

### A Proposed Solution: Okta for AI Agents

The video highlights **Okta's new platform, 'Okta for AI agents'**, announced at a showcase event. The platform's core idea is to treat every AI agent like an employee: it gets its own identity, specific permissions, and a **kill switch** to instantly shut it down if it behaves maliciously. This approach aims to bring the necessary governance and control to the rapidly expanding agent ecosystem.

The presenter predicts that companies will soon have **more agents than people**, driven by productivity gains rather than human replacement. In this future, the identity landscape will consist of human accounts, service accounts, and AI agent identities, all requiring proper management.

## Context

This video highlights a critical and often overlooked aspect of the AI revolution in business. As companies race to deploy AI agents for competitive advantage, they are inadvertently creating massive security vulnerabilities. It connects to broader trends of AI integration and the need for new security frameworks beyond traditional human-centric models.
