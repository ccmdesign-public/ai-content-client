## Key Takeaways

Microsoft's Bing Webmaster Tools update marks a significant step in AI search analytics, providing unprecedented visibility into how content is used by AI. Key insights include: • The new **AI performance dashboard** shows **total citations**, **visibility trends**, and **grounding queries**—the key phrases AI uses to retrieve content. • This data, though limited to Microsoft Copilot traffic, offers valuable **predictive insights** for broader AI search strategy, especially for B2B marketers. • The release sets a **precedent** that will increase pressure on Google and OpenAI to provide their own AI search analytics. • Marketers should **set up Bing Webmaster Tools** to explore this data and use existing **keyword research** and **AI tracking tools** to model AI search behavior.

## Summary

Microsoft has added a new AI performance tracking section to Bing Webmaster Tools, a first-of-its-kind analytics dashboard for AI search. This dashboard provides data specifically from **Microsoft Copilot** and its partners, offering insights previously unavailable.

The core metrics include **total citations** (how often your content is referenced as a source in AI answers), **visibility trends over time**, **page-level citation activity**, and most notably, **grounding queries**. **Grounding queries** are the key phrases the AI model uses when retrieving content to formulate an answer. This is analogous to traditional keyword data in Google Search Console but tailored for the longer, more conversational prompts typical of AI search.

While the immediate data set is small (limited to Bing and Copilot traffic), its strategic value is high. It provides a concrete look at the types of queries that trigger AI citations, which is invaluable for **B2B businesses** whose audiences often use Microsoft's ecosystem. The hosts emphasize that this move is likely to **pressure Google and OpenAI** to develop similar reporting features for their dominant platforms like Google AI Overviews and ChatGPT.

For marketers with little Bing traffic, the recommendation is to still set up Bing Webmaster Tools to see the new reports, but to primarily rely on a combined strategy: using traditional **keyword research tools** (like Semrush) to model likely AI search queries, and employing dedicated **AI search tracking platforms** (like Peak AI) to monitor visibility for a predefined set of prompts.

To gather data on what prompts customers are using, the hosts suggest several tactics: analyzing customer forums (Reddit, Quora), reviewing sales call transcripts with AI tools, and directly asking customers who mention finding you via an AI chatbot. The overarching message is that this Bing update is a critical first step in a new era of **AI search optimization (AISO)**, and marketers need to start building their understanding and data collection methods now.

## Context

This update is crucial for SEO professionals, digital marketers, and business owners as AI-powered search begins to reshape how users discover information and brands. Traditional SEO analytics are becoming less complete as a growing portion of searches happen within AI chatbots like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Copilot, which don't provide referral data. Microsoft's move to offer some transparency sets a new industry benchmark and forces the conversation about attribution and performance tracking in the AI search era. Anyone invested in organic online visibility needs to understand this shift.
