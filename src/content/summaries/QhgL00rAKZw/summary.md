## Key Takeaways

The core message is about solving the input-output problem for scaling a high-ticket service business: first optimize the funnel for quality, then scale the top of the funnel with targeted traffic. Key insights include: • **Friction increases quality, not quantity**: Adding application filters improved lead quality and close rates but reduced total volume. • **Scale quality traffic after fixing the funnel**: Use customer surveys to identify high-intent content and run paid ads on Meta/YouTube with 'save-worthy' reels/shorts. • **Retargeting and meta ads are key unlocks**: A compliant YouTube retargeting campaign and Meta ads (easier to start with) can drive qualified leads to the now-optimized sales process.

## Summary

Dr. Anne Trunk runs a sexual health medical practice generating $3.8M annually, aiming for $10M. Her primary traffic source is YouTube (150k subscribers), but she faces a lead qualification problem, not a traffic problem.

### The Funnel Fix: Adding Friction

Previously, too many unqualified leads were booking sales calls for high-ticket treatments ($15k-$20k). The team implemented an application funnel with disqualifying questions (e.g., insurance coverage, price transparency). This added **friction**, which successfully filtered out unqualified applicants. The result was a higher **percentage of qualified leads** on sales calls and an improved close rate, though the **absolute number** of leads decreased. Disqualified leads were down-sold to supplements or online coaching, but the focus remained on the high-value in-office treatments.

The consultant (Alex Hormozi) clarified the logic: by removing 50% of unqualified applicants upfront, the quality of the remaining leads increases. The mission was accomplished for the first phase—**optimizing the conversion process**.

### The Next Problem: Scaling Quality Traffic

With a high-converting funnel in place, the constraint shifted to generating more high-intent leads. Two primary solutions were proposed:
1.  **Improve Content Targeting**: Identify which types of existing YouTube content attract the best customers. Methods include:

*   Emailing current customers with a giveaway incentive to ask which specific video or content type brought them in.

*   Adding a question on the sales call: "What video got you here?"
    *   The goal is to create more content that caters to the **ideal customer avatar**, even if it has a smaller viewership than broader, general-interest content.
2.  **Leverage Paid Advertising**: The practice had previously run Meta ads but stopped due to low lead quality—a problem that existed *before* the funnel was fixed. The recommendation is to relaunch ads now that the sales process filters for quality.

*   **Retargeting**: First, fix compliance issues (the account was shut down by Google) and run YouTube retargeting ads to warm audiences who have already watched content.

*   **Meta & YouTube Prospecting**: Run paid ads on Meta and potentially YouTube to new audiences. Meta is suggested as an easier platform to start with, as the target demographic likely uses Instagram/Facebook.

*   **Ad Creative Strategy**: Use top-performing, **'save-worthy'** content—specifically Reels/Shorts that teach processes or steps—which indicates high purchase intent. Analyze the last year of content, find the top 10% with the highest saves-to-likes ratio, and use those for ads.

Since patients fly in from across the country, ads can be run nationally. The roadmap is clear: fix the funnel's conversion quality first, then systematically increase the volume of high-quality traffic entering it.

## Context

This case study is highly relevant for service-based businesses, consultants, coaches, and medical/legal practices selling high-ticket offers ($10k+). It demonstrates a critical scaling principle: you must solve for conversion quality *before* scaling traffic, otherwise you waste resources and overwhelm your sales team. The discussion connects to broader trends in direct-response marketing, funnel optimization, and using owned media (YouTube) as a foundation for paid customer acquisition.
