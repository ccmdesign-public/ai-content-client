*   **Don't stop a working flow**: If the current Meta ads are generating a positive return (3:1), they should not be turned off; instead, new strategies should be layered on top to increase total revenue.

## Summary

The video features a consultation with a B2B lead generation agency that is generating a high volume of leads but is dissatisfied with their quality, as they are attracting low-salary, junior profiles. The agency's main constraint is lead quality, not quantity, and they are considering shifting their $10,000 monthly ad spend from Meta to platforms like LinkedIn or Twitter to target higher-salary executives.

The advisor strongly recommends against stopping the current Meta ad campaigns, as they are profitable with a 3:1 return on investment (ROI). Instead, the agency should **add a performance-based pricing component** to their existing Meta ads. This single change is predicted to significantly boost the ROI, potentially from 3:1 to 10:1 or even 12:1, effectively increasing the customer lifetime value (LTV) from $3,000 to $10,000-$12,000.

The core advice is to **improve the ROI of the current profitable channel first** before testing new ones. The advisor suggests that by improving the ad creative and adding performance incentives, the agency can overcome diminishing returns and potentially scale their Meta ad spend much higher while maintaining a strong return, rather than abandoning a channel that already works.
