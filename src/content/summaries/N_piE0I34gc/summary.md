## Key Takeaways

Google DeepMind has achieved a breakthrough in mathematical AI research. • **Althia**, a long-horizon research agent, solved six open PhD-level problems in the First Proof Challenge with zero human intervention. • Its dual-role **generator/verifier architecture** ensures reliability by constantly debating ideas and rejecting unsound proofs. • The system demonstrates **mathematical AGI**—capable of discovering new structure and reasoning over long chains of ideas like a human researcher.

## Summary

Google DeepMind's AI agent **Althia** has achieved what researchers call **mathematical AGI** by independently solving six open, PhD-level mathematical research problems submitted to the **First Proof Challenge**. This is considered more significant than winning a gold medal at the International Mathematical Olympiad, as these problems sit at the research frontier with no known solution paths.

Althia is a **long-horizon research agent** built on top of DeepMind's **Gemini 3 Deep Think System**. It's designed for sustained reasoning over thousands of potential steps, including failed attempts. A key innovation is its internal **generator/verifier architecture**, where one part aggressively proposes ideas and the other ruthlessly attacks them, ensuring the system never produces plausible but incorrect proofs. For the four problems it didn't solve, it correctly reported 'no solution found' or remained silent.

### Problem 7: A Case Study

One problem, unsolved for years in algebraic topology and differential geometry, was solved by Althia in **two completely different ways**. The first proof used a contradiction based on **Lefschetz numbers**, while the second employed geometric mapping and the **Cartan fixed point theorem**. The reasoning chain was clean and combined deep theorems naturally. Solving this single problem required **16 times the computational budget** of a previous flagship AI math result (Airdos 151), demonstrating sustained exploration and backtracking.

### Broader Implications and Infrastructure

This marks the potential end of the 'manual era' of mathematical research. AI agents like Althia don't tire or get attached to bad ideas; they grind through possibilities relentlessly. Concurrently, Google announced a massive renewable energy data center in Minnesota, featuring a **300-megawatt iron-air battery system** from **Form Energy** capable of 100-hour storage. This infrastructure is critical because running advanced AI research agents at scale is energy-intensive. Google is building both the smarter AI and the **sustainable power infrastructure** needed to support its future development.

## Context

This achievement represents a fundamental shift in AI capabilities, moving beyond pattern recognition in language or games to genuine discovery in a rigorous, structured domain like pure mathematics. It matters to researchers, technologists, and anyone tracking the progression toward artificial general intelligence (AGI). The development connects AI advancement directly to the massive computational and energy infrastructure required to sustain it, highlighting a critical path for scalable, responsible AI development.
