* **Automated lead scraping** allows you to gather hundreds of targeted profiles in minutes.

* **Customizable search criteria** like job title, country, language, and city help you find your ideal leads.

## Summary

The video presents a workflow system designed to automate the initial stages of LinkedIn lead generation. The host demonstrates the process by filling out a form with specific criteria to find potential leads.

**The Process**
The user starts by entering search parameters into a form. In the demonstration, the host searches for "marketing manager" in the United States, sets the language to English, selects New York as the city, and requests a "list count" of 20 leads. Upon submitting the form, an automated workflow is triggered.

**Data Collection**
The system then scrapes LinkedIn, inserting leads one by one into a structured output. For each profile found, the system captures multiple data points into individual fields. The collected information includes the person's full name, their specific job title, the name of their company, and their direct LinkedIn profile URL.

**Additional Metrics**
Beyond basic contact info, the tool also gathers valuable engagement metrics. It records the number of connections a person has and their follower count. It also captures a "row snippet" from the search engine results (SER), providing contextual information about the lead.

**Use Case & Outreach**
This automated data collection serves as a powerful foundation for sales and marketing outreach. With a populated list of targeted leads, users can then efficiently send connection requests. The host suggests using the collected data to craft personalized "icebreaker" messages, enabling users to connect with people in their specific niche or with their ideal customer profile.

## Context

For sales professionals, marketers, and business developers, finding and connecting with the right people on LinkedIn is time-consuming and manual. This tool addresses that pain point by automating lead discovery, which is crucial for scaling outreach efforts and improving sales pipeline efficiency. It connects to the broader trend of using automation and data scraping for business development, allowing individuals and small teams to compete with larger organizations that have dedicated research staff.
