## Key Takeaways

OpenAI is shifting ChatGPT's role in e-commerce, while Google is doubling down on in-AI purchasing. This creates distinct opportunities for businesses:

*   **ChatGPT's focus on discovery and research** means businesses should optimize for informational content and digital PR to appear in its results, driving high-converting top-of-funnel traffic.

*   **Google's aggressive push into agentic commerce** with features like in-AI cart, loyalty programs, and one-click account creation necessitates technical SEO and immediate integration for large e-commerce players.

*   **Building user trust** is crucial for in-AI purchasing, a challenge OpenAI faced, but Google benefits from its established user base and payment infrastructure.

*   E-commerce businesses, especially smaller ones, should prioritize **optimizing for AI search discovery** for their high-profit products, rather than immediately chasing in-AI checkout.

## Summary

This discussion centers on the evolving landscape of AI in e-commerce, particularly the contrasting strategies of OpenAI's ChatGPT and Google's AI search. OpenAI is reportedly **pulling back from instant checkout functionality directly within ChatGPT**. Instead, they are shifting towards connecting retailers' apps to ChatGPT, funneling users through to external platforms rather than completing purchases in-chat.

### OpenAI's Strategy Shift

The likely reason for this change is a lower-than-expected uptake and the inherent challenge of **building consumer trust** for direct purchases within a new AI platform. Consumers are accustomed to buying from established retailers like Amazon or trusted brand websites. While ChatGPT can effectively handle top-of-funnel conversations, such as product research and discovery, users tend to complete purchases on familiar sites. This is evidenced by higher conversion rates for traffic coming from ChatGPT for complex, high-ticket items like windows, suggesting its strength lies in the **informational and consideration stages** of the buying journey.

OpenAI appears to be focusing on areas where ChatGPT already demonstrates success: driving highly qualified traffic to e-commerce sites. Businesses should leverage ChatGPT for **informational content, digital PR, and user-generated content** to influence the discovery phase, particularly for their high-profit and hero products. This approach aims to dominate the top and middle of the funnel in ChatGPT conversations.

### Google's Agentic Commerce Push

In stark contrast, Google is significantly **expanding its instant checkout and agentic commerce capabilities** within its AI mode and search results. New features include:

*   **In-AI cart functionality** allowing users to add multiple items from the same shop.

*   **Catalog displays** to browse full product ranges from retailers like Lowe's.

*   Integration with **loyalty programs**, ensuring users still earn points even when purchasing directly through Google's AI interface.

*   **One-click account creation** with Google accounts and seamless integration with Google Wallet for payment.

Google benefits from its established user trust and existing payment infrastructure. This allows them to push for a complete in-AI shopping experience. Agentic commerce, where AI agents facilitate transactions, is seen as the future of e-commerce, and Google, as a dominant player, is positioned to lead this shift. Businesses, especially large market leaders, need to engage with Google's **Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP)** and optimize for these advanced AI search features.

### Implications for E-commerce Businesses

E-commerce businesses face the challenge of optimizing for both AI search discovery and agentic commerce. For smaller businesses, the immediate focus should be on **technical SEO, clear content strategy, and digital PR** to ensure visibility in AI search results, particularly for high-profit products. This helps capture the high-converting, top-of-funnel traffic that AI platforms like ChatGPT can drive.

Larger enterprises with more resources should pursue a dual strategy: optimizing for both discovery in AI tools and integrating with agentic commerce platforms. The goal is to **saturate the search engine results page**, appearing in ads, AI overviews, and high organic positions to own the entire customer funnel.

## Context

The rapid evolution of AI and its integration into search and e-commerce platforms is fundamentally changing how consumers discover and purchase products. This discussion highlights a critical divergence in strategy between two major AI players, OpenAI and Google, regarding in-chat e-commerce. Understanding these shifts is crucial for e-commerce businesses and marketers to adapt their strategies, optimize their online presence, and effectively engage with consumers in an AI-driven landscape. It addresses the challenge of building trust in new purchasing environments and the strategic importance of different stages of the customer journey within AI interactions.
