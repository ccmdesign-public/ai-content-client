### Growth Strategy & Funding

The discussion revolves around the optimal strategy to achieve this rapid growth. The founder initially considers raising investment for a new, scalable platform currently under development. However, the mentor advises against this, emphasizing that the company's current **50-60% profit margins** (even after heavy investment in growth, including developer salaries) provide sufficient **internal cash flow** to fund development.

Instead, the mentor strongly advocates for focusing on **influencer marketing** and **relationship building** with key creators. The core idea is to identify and partner with a larger number of highly influential TikTok Shop creators who can significantly amplify the agency's brand and drive audience to its platform. This approach is seen as a more **cost-effective** and **high-leverage** growth strategy compared to relying on external capital, which can sometimes lead to a "money makes you lazy" mindset.

The agency's revenue model is a blend of **membership fees** (priced at $50 and $200) and **transaction fees** from successful brand-creator matchmaking. Currently, revenue is split roughly 50/50 between these two streams. This diversified model is considered robust and effective. While the agency is building a full-scale platform to enhance scalability, the mentor suggests that the blended model is acceptable and the immediate focus should be on maximizing the impact of influential creators.

In essence, the advice is to double down on what is already working exceptionally well – leveraging the power of creators and community – to drive exponential growth without external financial dependence.
