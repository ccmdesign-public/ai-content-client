## Key Takeaways

This video presents a strategic framework for brands to build authentic presence on Reddit without appearing as spammers. • **Phase 1 (Crawl)**: Spend weeks 1-4 learning Reddit culture in 3-5 relevant subreddits, posting only 2-3 helpful comments weekly to build karma. • **Phase 2 (Walk)**: During months 2-3, answer questions in your expertise using the phrase 'In an effort to add value, here's what I learned' before sharing insights. • **Phase 3 (Run)**: From month 4 onward, create branded subreddits, run AMAs, coordinate with SEO teams, and run ads once you understand the platform culture.

## Summary

Most brands get banned from Reddit within 24 hours because they approach the platform with traditional marketing tactics. Reddit's community-driven culture requires a fundamentally different approach focused on building **karma** (Reddit's reputation system) before attempting any promotion.

The **three-phase framework** provides a structured timeline for success. **Phase one: Crawl** (weeks one through four) has a single goal: don't get banned while learning the culture. Brands should identify 3-5 subreddits where their target audience hangs out, read top posts to understand communication styles, and begin commenting with 2-3 genuinely helpful comments per week—absolutely no promotion, just adding value to build karma slowly.

**Phase two: Walk** (months two and three) focuses on building credibility through expertise sharing. The recommended approach is to answer questions in your area of expertise using the magic phrase: 'In an effort to add value, here's what I learned.' This frames your contribution as helpful rather than promotional. Only if your product naturally fits as part of the solution should you mention it at this stage.

**Phase three: Run** (months four and beyond) allows for more active brand building once you've established credibility. Options include creating a branded subreddit if there's genuine community demand, running Ask Me Anything (AMA) sessions with founders or team members, coordinating Reddit content with SEO teams to align with blog content, and finally running paid ads with the cultural understanding needed to make them effective.

## Context

Reddit represents a massive opportunity for brands with over 430 million monthly active users, but its anti-commercial culture makes traditional marketing approaches ineffective and often results in immediate bans. This framework matters because it provides a systematic approach to building authentic community presence rather than treating Reddit as just another advertising channel. Marketing professionals, community managers, and startup founders should care about this approach to avoid wasting resources and damaging brand reputation through inappropriate platform engagement.
