## Key Takeaways

The core advice is to simplify pricing and focus on relationship continuity for a luxury service business. Key insights include: • **Abandon the complex pricing ladder** for a simple, bespoke core offer. • **Implement a low-cost annual continuity plan** (like a maintenance retainer) to stay top-of-mind and secure future work. • **Price for value, not cost-per-unit**; ultra-high-net-worth clients prioritize quality and trust over minor price differences. • **Start with smaller jobs** to build trust that leads to larger, whole-home projects over time.

## Summary

In this consultation, Alex Hormozi critiques a business owner's proposed three-tier pricing structure for her interior design and wellness advisory service targeting ultra-high-net-worth families. The current model included Tier 1 at $80/sq ft for a single home, Tier 2 at $100/sq ft for a 7-10 year portfolio commitment, and Tier 3 at $100/sq ft plus a $200k annual retainer for full ecosystem management (yachts, planes, offices).

Hormozi's primary critique is that the proposed **pricing ladder is undifferentiated and overly complex**. The price jumps between tiers are too small (only 20-25%) to justify the perceived value leap, and long-term commitments feel heavy for clients who value flexibility. He argues the business is inherently **bespoke**, and trying to force it into rigid tiers doesn't align with how high-net-worth clients buy.

### The Recommended Strategy

Hormozi advises a complete pivot:
1.  **Simplify the core offer**: Use a single, clean price point—suggesting **$100 per square foot**—for the primary service of designing a wellness-focused home. This is easy for clients to understand and calculate.
2.  **Add a strategic continuity plan**: Instead of a high-pressure, long-term contract, introduce a low-cost **annual retainer** (e.g., a few thousand dollars). Position this as 'insurance' or a wellness check-up for the home. This plan serves three critical functions:

*   It guarantees an annual touchpoint to maintain the relationship.

*   It provides a reason to be on-site, where you can naturally discover and discuss the client's next major project (a new home, yacht, etc.).

*   It creates predictable revenue and keeps the business top-of-mind during 'off' years between large projects.
3.  **Price projects bespokely**: For non-standard assets like yachts or jets, create separate, tailored pricing rather than trying to bundle them into a tier. The continuity plan ensures you're the first call when these needs arise.

Hormozi emphasizes that for this clientele, **perceived value and trust far outweigh minor cost differences**. The goal is to become the trusted, go-to advisor for all property-related needs across generations, which is achieved through consistent excellence and relationship nurturing, not complex pricing schemes.
