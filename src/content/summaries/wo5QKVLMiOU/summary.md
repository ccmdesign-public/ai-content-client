## Key Takeaways

The video explains why 'be concise' fails with AI and presents a three-layer system for getting shorter, more direct answers.

- **Define terms specifically**: Instead of 'be concise,' specify exact response lengths (1-2 sentences, 3-5 sentences, one paragraph with 5 bullets) in **system instructions** for custom GPTs or projects.

- **Force structured formats**: During prompting, add constraints like 'answer only as a comparison table' or 'list with 5 items max' to eliminate filler content automatically.

- **Sharpen after generation**: Post-response, instruct the AI to 'cut length by 50%, remove generalizations, use short punchy sentences' or filter lengthy deep research reports by extracting only actionable findings in a new thread.

## Summary

AI models often produce verbose responses because they're trained on internet data where longer answers receive higher human ratings, reinforcing wordiness as a 'rewarded' behavior. This wastes time and can degrade the AI's contextual intelligence over a conversation as its 'head' fills with fluff.

### The Three-Layer System

**Layer 1: Before Interaction (Setting Rules)**
Set ground rules in the AI's system instructions (available in custom GPTs, Gemini Gems, or Claude Projects).

- **Define 'Concise' Explicitly**: Replace vague commands with specific directives. Example: 'For simple questions: respond with 1-2 sentences. For medium: 3-5 sentences. For complex: one paragraph summary plus a maximum of five bullet points.'
- **Use Positive, Directive Language**: Instead of 'don't use filler,' say 'start directly with the answer, use short declarative sentences, lead with the most important point.' This avoids the 'purple elephant' effect where negations can backfire.

**Layer 2: During Interaction (Prompting)**
Integrate formatting constraints directly into your prompt to force concise outputs.

- **Force a Specific Format**: Append instructions like 'answer only as a comparison table with these rows: Cost, Speed, Control' to your query. This mechanically prevents the AI from adding preamble, hedging, or closing statements.

- **Alternative Formats**: You can also request matrices, capped lists (e.g., 'list 3 items, max 10 words each'), or step-by-step guides with sentence limits per step.

**Layer 3: After Response (Refinement)**
Refine the AI's output after you receive it.

- **Immediate Sharpening**: Reply to a lengthy response with: 'Cut the length by 50%, remove all generalizations, and provide back short, punchy sentences only.'
- **Filter Pass for Deep Research**: For features like 'Deep Research' that generate 20-40 page reports, copy the output into a new chat with a different AI and command it to 'extract only the specific, actionable findings. Remove all background context, general principles, and introductory material.'

**Important Caveat**: The video recommends avoiding 'Deep Research' for most use cases. Instead, using **basic web search with extended reasoning** on high-end models often yields 90% of the quality at 10% of the length.

### Recap of the Workflow

1.  **Before**: Bake specific length definitions and positive instructions into system prompts.
2.  **During**: Constrain the output format within your live prompt (tables, lists).
3.  **After**: Sharpen responses by cutting length or filtering out fluff from large reports.

## Context

As AI assistants like ChatGPT and Claude become ubiquitous, a common frustration is their tendency to generate unnecessarily long, fluffy responses. This happens because AI models are trained on internet data where verbosity is often rewarded, and they lack a user-specific understanding of 'conciseness.' For professionals, entrepreneurs, and anyone using AI for work, wading through verbose outputs wastes significant time and can reduce the effectiveness of the AI over extended conversations. This video provides a systematic, practical framework to solve this core productivity problem in human-AI interaction.
