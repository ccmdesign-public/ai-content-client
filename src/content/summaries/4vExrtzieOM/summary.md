## Key Takeaways

The video argues that $10M/year is achievable in any business through persistence and market expansion. • **Stick with your business**: Switching costs reset progress, and growth accelerates with size, making commitment more effective than starting over. • **Understand business model shapes**: Each model (e.g., SaaS, e-commerce, service) has unique growth curves and challenges that are features, not bugs. • **Double down on your opportunity**: Success comes from fully committing to overcome inherent difficulties rather than constantly seeking new ventures.

## Summary

The video emphasizes that reaching $10 million in annual revenue is possible in virtually any business, except for extremely limited local brick-and-mortar markets. The primary strategy is to expand into new markets rather than abandoning your current venture.

**The cost of switching** is a critical concept. If you switch to a new business, even if it starts with faster growth, you lose the head start from your original venture. Growth becomes easier as you scale, so sticking with your current business builds momentum and accelerates progress over time.

Different **business models** have distinct growth patterns and inherent challenges:

- **Info/education businesses**: Start fast with quick revenue but are hard to scale due to low revenue retention, as customers learn and move on.

- **SaaS (software) businesses**: Start slow with initial losses but scale rapidly once established, requiring patience and investment.

- **E-commerce businesses**: Can scale quickly but face cash flow and supply chain issues, such as inventory management and 3PL logistics.

- **Service businesses**: Grow slowly and steadily, with challenges in talent acquisition and retention, varying by industry.

These challenges are **features, not bugs** – they are inherent aspects that define the business and justify compensation. For example, cleaning businesses struggle with attracting talent, while fitness businesses face customer retention issues. The speaker uses a **marriage analogy** to illustrate that doubling down on your current opportunity, like working on a relationship, is more efficient than constantly seeking new ones. By understanding your business model's nature and committing persistently, you can scale to $10 million per year through market expansion and overcoming these features.

## Context

This video is crucial for entrepreneurs and business owners who feel stuck or tempted to switch ventures in pursuit of faster growth. It addresses common impatience and the misconception that other opportunities are easier, emphasizing that most financial goals are achievable within existing businesses through commitment and strategic expansion. In an era where new shiny objects constantly distract, it promotes focused execution and resilience as key to scaling success.
