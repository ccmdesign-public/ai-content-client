The course is carefully structured into four main phases to ensure a smooth learning curve [1].

**Fundamentals**

Students begin with the building blocks of AI [1]. This section covers what ChatGPT is, how Large Language Models operate, the concepts of tokens and tokenization, and how temperature and sampling parameters shape AI responses [1]. It also includes training on writing effective prompts [1].

**Architecture**

The second phase transitions into system assembly [1]. Learners explore how AI systems are built from pods, what tools are, and how to integrate them [1]. A crucial topic in this section is understanding the distinct difference between an AI workflow and a true AI agent [1].

**Implementation**

In this practical phase, students learn the core components of agent creation [1]. Topics include agent memory, planning and reasoning strategies, multi-agent systems, and error handling patterns [1]. It also explores production-level design patterns utilized by industry leaders like Anthropic and Google [1].

**Case Study: OpenClaw**

The course concludes with an in-depth dissection of OpenClaw, a popular open-source AI agent [1]. Additionally, the section addresses the security challenges and concerns surrounding OpenClaw, providing a comprehensive view of real-world AI agent deployment [1].
