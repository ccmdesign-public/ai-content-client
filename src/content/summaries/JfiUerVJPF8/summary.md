## Key Takeaways

The core advice for scaling a private equity firm centers on strategic focus and talent acquisition. Key insights include focusing on **fewer, larger deals** to maximize bandwidth and returns, being in the **talent business** by paying 90th percentile salaries to attract 'savages,' and **pulling key performers** from smaller portfolio companies to work across the entire holding company for greater impact.

## Summary

The video features a discussion where an experienced operator advises the founder of a $25M/year private equity firm on scaling to a billion-dollar enterprise value. The founder's current model involves deal-by-deal funding from family offices but lacks the institutional capability for significant scale.

The advisor identifies a common scaling trap: firms often raise more capital but continue doing the same number of deals, just at larger sizes, without increasing their operational bandwidth. The critical shift is to move from many small deals to **fewer, bigger deals** where the firm can genuinely generate alpha (superior returns).

A major theme is redefining the firm's core business. The advisor states, "we're in the talent business 100%." The primary scaling mechanism is aggressively recruiting and retaining top performers. This involves a willingness to **overpay significantly**, such as paying a sales director $400,000 instead of the market rate of $200,000, with the expectation of generating millions in extra return.

The advisor shares a powerful tactic from his own experience at Acquisition.com: **extracting 'diamonds' from portfolio companies**. When acquiring a small business, the real value might not be the company itself but one or two exceptional individuals (e.g., a marketer or sales leader). The strategy is to pull these high-performers into the holding company (holdco), where their skills can be leveraged across the entire portfolio, creating far more value than they could in a single, small deal.

Operational scaling requires building institutional capability. This includes having a **director of talent** with executive recruiting experience and maintaining a recruiting team nearly the size of the sales team. The goal is to systematically identify talent, either by pulling winners out of acquisitions or inserting winning teams into portfolio companies to drive growth.

## Context

This discussion is crucial for entrepreneurs, business owners, and investors looking to scale an acquisition-based or private equity model beyond the founder's direct involvement. It addresses the universal challenge of moving from a hands-on, deal-by-deal operation to a scalable institution with repeatable processes and leveraged talent. The insights apply to anyone building a portfolio of companies or aiming to systemize growth beyond personal bandwidth.
