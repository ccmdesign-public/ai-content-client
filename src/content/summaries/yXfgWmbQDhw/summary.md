## Summary

The video creator hosts the Silicon Valley Girl podcast, interviewing major tech figures like Reed Hoffman, Mustafa Sullean, and Priscilla Chan. For each episode, the team needs B-roll footage to make the conversations visually engaging, but traditional methods were inefficient.

Previously, the content creation process involved hours of digging through stock footage or recording custom videos that never quite matched the interview topics. This was the slowest part of their entire content workflow.

The breakthrough came with **Free Pick Spaces**, an AI tool that completely transformed their B-roll creation process. The workflow begins with **Cream50**, a new image model from Bite Dance, where the creator types specific prompts describing the needed footage.

For example, prompts like "Futuristic AI research lab, soft blue lighting, cinematic wide angle" generate clean, intentional frames. The model doesn't create random images—it actually considers composition and depth before generating content.

What makes Free Pick Spaces unique is its integrated workflow. After generating an image, the creator connects it to a video node within the same workspace, and the still frame transforms into moving B-roll footage. This eliminates the need to switch between different applications or deal with downloading and uploading files between tools.

The creator shows real examples of clips generated for actual episodes, including footage for conversations about AI in healthcare and episodes about the future of search. All generated content matches the tone and theme of the interviews while coming from the same streamlined workflow.

The time savings are dramatic: what used to take the team a full day now takes about 15 minutes. This efficiency gain allows for more consistent, thematically relevant B-roll that enhances viewer engagement without the traditional production bottlenecks.
