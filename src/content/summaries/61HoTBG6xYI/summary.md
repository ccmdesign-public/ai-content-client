## Summary

AI capabilities are advancing at an unprecedented pace, turning 'impossible' tasks into trivial ones within months. The presenter shares a personal example where extracting data from complex construction PDFs—which previously required five weeks of engineering and six different AI models—now works perfectly on the first try with Gemini 3 Flash using a basic prompt.

Three specific capabilities have recently crossed the **'not yet' to 'just works' threshold**:
• **Complex PDF Data Extraction**: Handling PDFs with intricate tables, handwriting, and embedded images is now reliable for industries like construction, legal, and insurance.
• **Image Consistency**: Maintaining consistent logos, products, and character appearances across generated images (critical for branding and ads) has improved dramatically with newer models.
• **Handwriting Extraction**: Accuracy for extracting handwritten notes from photos has jumped from ~60% to near 100%, making it practical for medical forms, meeting notes, and documentation.

### Systematic Retesting Framework
To avoid being **'too early'** (wasting time on unsolved problems) or **'too late'** (missing competitive advantages), implement this three-step system:

1. **Create an AI Wish List**: Document tasks AI couldn't handle with three columns: Task, Date Tested, and Result. Store this in Google Docs, Apple Notes, or similar.
2. **Set Retest Triggers**: Retest your list when:

- A new model launches from **OpenAI, Anthropic, or Google**
   - A relevant new feature is released

- Your **quarterly calendar reminder** triggers
3. **Follow Testing Protocol**:

- Test with a simple prompt first

- If it fails, improve **prompt quality, context, and specific expectations**
   - Only after optimizing inputs should you conclude the task still isn't viable

This systematic approach ensures you identify capability breakthroughs as soon as they happen, giving you months of advantage over competitors who revisit AI capabilities less frequently.
