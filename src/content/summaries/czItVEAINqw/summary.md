*   **The 90-Day, Two-Person Launch:** Start with a core team of a **builder** (who can go from idea to product in 24 hours) and a **go-to-market expert** (who deeply understands the target audience and can create viral social content). Aim for first revenue by day 30 and $1M annual run rate (ARR) by day 90.

Instead, find verticals (e.g., property management, specific marketing workflows) where AI can solve a high-frequency, high-value problem for customers willing to pay ~$2,000/month. Focus on **Average Contract Value (ACV)** and **Daily Active Users (DAU)**, not just vanity metrics.

## Summary

### The 90-Day AI Startup Blueprint

Alex, the founder of Higgsfield AI, presents a radically accelerated framework for building a business in the current AI landscape. He asserts that the barrier to creating a million-dollar company has never been lower, advocating for a focused, two-person team to launch within 90 days. The ideal founding team consists of a **technical builder** capable of turning an idea into a minimum viable product (MVP) within 24 hours, leveraging modern databases and payment systems, and a **go-to-market expert** with deep empathy for the target audience and a knack for creating compelling social media content. The goal is to generate first revenue by day 30 and reach a $1 million annual run rate (ARR) by day 90.

### The Higgsfield Case Study: Speed and Customer Empathy

Higgsfield's own journey to $200 million in annual recurring revenue within nine months serves as the primary case study. A key lesson was the critical importance of **daily iteration**. The company shipped new product releases six days a week, constantly testing workflows and use cases to find product-market fit. This rapid pace is necessitated by the industry itself, which "resets" every month as leading research labs push major model updates, often requiring substantial product rebuilds.

The pivotal moment for Higgsfield came from direct, unfiltered customer feedback. After initial struggles with mobile apps, they interviewed eight creative professionals—from Hollywood directors to regional commercial producers—and all eight identified the same core limitation: a lack of fine-grained **camera control** in AI video generation. This insight, gathered from a small but perfectly targeted sample, led to the feature that drove their initial traction. They later hired four of those interviewees, creating a powerful feedback loop. Today, roughly 40% of Higgsfield's team are creators, embodying a symbiosis between engineers and end-users.

### Defensibility in the Age of AI Giants

A major concern for new founders is competition from well-funded giants like OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, and Microsoft. Alex argues that defensibility comes from a **nuanced, vertical-specific approach**. While large companies aim to provide AI to everyone, startups must identify and deeply serve specific industries where they can deliver exceptional, tailored value. He gives the example of a large property management company using Higgsfield—a niche no large AI lab is building for, but where automating the customer journey with AI agents can directly impact revenue.

He advises founders to focus on building **cash-flow positive businesses from day one**, rather than automatically seeking venture capital. The goal should be to serve "tens of millions of users who are willing to spend hopefully a couple thousand dollars a month." Key metrics are **Daily Active Users (DAU)** and **Average Contract Value (ACV)**, not inflated monthly active user counts. For distribution, he notes that for AI products, Twitter/X has been the primary launchpad, where hype originates in small communities before spreading to AI news pages and then to other social platforms like Instagram and Telegram.

### AI as a Tool for Personal and Professional Ascent

Alex frames AI as the next great **social elevator**, comparable to how social media created opportunities in the previous decade. It is particularly empowering for young, non-traditional builders and creators. For individuals feeling left behind, his advice is to personally embrace AI to augment their skill set. He shares that **Gemini 3 Pro** has become a critical tool for his own economic productivity, handling deep reasoning and research, while he focuses his personal energy on human-centric skills like communication, conflict resolution, and goal-setting—areas where AI is still weak.

He concludes by addressing creator anxiety about AI-generated content, arguing that while AI will democratize the creation of average content, **authenticity and a deep understanding of one's audience will matter more than ever**. The winners will be those who use AI to supercharge their unique voice and scale their authentic connection, potentially building the next media empires.
