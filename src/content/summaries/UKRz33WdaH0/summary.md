Google is advancing multimodal AI with new consumer-facing tools. Key developments include:

*   **Lyria 3** now generates complete music tracks with vocals directly in Gemini, accepting text, image, or video prompts.

*   **Pomelli Photoshoot** enables AI-powered product photo generation for marketing, streamlining asset creation for businesses.

## Summary

Google has launched **Lyria 3**, its latest music generation model, marking a significant step from research to consumer product. It is now available in the Gemini app and powers YouTube's Dream Track. The model generates 30-second, high-quality (48 kHz, 16-bit stereo) music tracks complete with vocals and instrumentation based on natural language prompts describing genre, mood, tempo, and even lyric language. Crucially, it accepts multimodal inputs: users can upload an image or video, and Lyria 3 will generate a matching soundtrack.

Google addresses copyright concerns by embedding an imperceptible **SynthID watermark** into every generated track, detectable even after compression or recording. The model is part of a broader **Music AI Sandbox** offering tools for musicians, and a **Lyria Real-Time** version allows for live, steerable music generation with under 2-second latency.

Alongside this, Google is expanding its AI tooling in other creative domains. **Pomelli**, Google's AI marketing platform, is rolling out **Photoshoot**, a feature that generates professional product photography from a single uploaded image, targeting SMBs and e-commerce. **Stitch**, the AI design tool, is introducing new agents like **Hatter**, designed for complex, multi-step design tasks, and features like automatic **app store asset generation**. Stitch now also offers native **Model Context Protocol (MCP)** integration, allowing designs to be exported directly to code editors like Cursor and Claude Code.

The overarching trend is Google's shift from releasing standalone models to building integrated systems. Lyria 3, Pomelli, and Stitch represent a converging stack where music, image generation, and design workflows are becoming seamless parts of a larger creative and productive ecosystem.

## Context

This update matters because it signals Google's aggressive push to integrate advanced AI capabilities directly into widely used consumer and business products, moving beyond demos. Creators, musicians, marketers, and developers should pay attention as these tools lower the barrier to high-quality content creation and streamline professional workflows. It reflects the broader industry trend of AI becoming a practical, multimodal tool embedded in everyday applications, with Google directly competing with specialized players like Suno and Udio in music AI.
