## Key Takeaways

The core advice is to maximize an existing, profitable channel before exploring new ones. The key insights are: • **Optimize the current B2B affiliate model** before pursuing B2C; the $500 acquisition cost for a $6,000 referral is a 12:1 ROI. • **Systematize affiliate activation** using the 'launch and integrate' model: treat the first referral as a trial, then run campaigns to convert the affiliate. • **Focus on expansion by replicating the playbook**; once the B2B process is refined in one market, replicate it in new cities rather than changing strategies.

## Summary

The video features a consultation with Mike Nathan, founder of a mobile cellular therapy business doing $1M in revenue with 50% margins. His goal is to scale to $25M and exit. The business acquires patients through B2B referrals from physicians, chiropractors, and physical therapists, with a cost of $500 per affiliate doctor who refers an average of one $6,000 patient per year.

The primary constraint identified is not the acquisition model itself, which has a strong 12:1 return, but the **lack of systematic activation and follow-up**. The founder has contacted 'less than a thousand' potential affiliates in his home market but gets only sporadic referrals because the relationship isn't managed for ongoing promotion.

The recommended strategy is the **'launch and integrate' model** for affiliate marketing. The 'launch' phase involves treating the first patient referral as a trial and then proactively proposing a coordinated campaign to the affiliate to promote the service to their entire patient base. The long-term 'integrate' goal is to make the referral so seamless it becomes part of the affiliate's standard process.

Critical execution steps include having a dedicated **affiliate manager** to consistently 'stoke the fire' through personal visits and reminders, and creating a **systematized feedback loop**. After a patient is treated, the business should call the referring doctor to report the successful outcome, which naturally opens the conversation for more referrals.

The advisor strongly argues against pivoting to a B2C online advertising strategy at this stage. The reasoning is that the existing B2B channel is already profitable and fundamentally unoptimized. The advice is to 'crush' and fully exploit the current playbook—getting each of the 200+ existing affiliates to refer multiple patients per month—before allocating resources to build a new, unproven customer acquisition channel. For geographic expansion, the refined B2B playbook should be replicated in new markets.
