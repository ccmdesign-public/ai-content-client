## Summary

The video introduces the concept of **LLM psychosis**, a psychological dependence on large language models that can impair leadership. By 2026, stable leaders will be defined by their ability to know when to shut down AI tools like ChatGPT and engage in human dialogue for decision-making.

Unstable leaders, in contrast, may rely excessively on AI, leading to a false sense of superiority and conflict with colleagues. This over-reliance risks business outcomes, as AI lacks common sense and human intuition.

The discussion references cases like David Buden's claim about solving the Navier-Stokes equations, where mathematical experts expressed skepticism. This illustrates the need for leaders to seek peer validation and not blindly trust AI-generated insights.

Businesses are recognizing this threat and are beginning to develop **quarterly tests** to assess leaders for undue AI influence. These tests aim to ensure that leaders maintain balance, using AI as a tool without becoming psychologically dependent.

Ultimately, the video stresses that AI is transformative but must be used responsibly. Leaders must remember that **AI plus human** is just a human with a tool, and effective work requires collaboration with other humans.
