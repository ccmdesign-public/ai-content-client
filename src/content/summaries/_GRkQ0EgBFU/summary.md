* **Deal box criteria**: Target $3M+ revenue, $1M+ EBITDA companies with established product-market fit in consumer/business services

* **Ideal target**: Companies with excellent products but incompetent sales/marketing where Hormozi's team can apply proven playbooks

## Summary

Alex Hormozi outlines his firm's disciplined approach to acquiring and scaling companies through Acquisition.com.

### Deal Selection Criteria

The firm has strict filters: companies must have at least $3 million in revenue and $1 million in EBITDA, indicating they've achieved basic product-market fit. They focus on consumer or business services (brick-and-mortar chains, national services) where they can see clear 5-10x improvement potential.

Hormozi looks for "specifically incompetent" founders

- those who have built great products with high customer lifetime value but struggle with sales, marketing, or scaling. As an example, they bought a 32-location teeth whitening chain where the product was excellent but the sales process was poor. After implementing their sales playbook, they 5xed the LTV in one rollout.

### Deal Flow and Screening Process

All deal flow (3,000 companies/month) comes from content

- people who have consumed hours of Hormozi's content across podcasts, books, and courses. This creates pre-educated sellers who have often already fixed low-hanging fruit.

The screening process has multiple layers:

* Automated screening eliminates 90% based on size, industry, or location

* Qualification calls verify data accuracy

* Business case analysis with one-page write-ups

* Managing director review leading to about 2 LOIs per month

### Scaling Methodology

The firm operates with specialized subject matter experts (SMEs) in IT, finance, HR, recruiting, marketing (both technical and creative), sales, and customer success. Each SME has experience scaling teams larger than most companies they acquire.

They've developed codified playbooks through experience across portfolio companies. Currently, they have three primary playbooks that can 10x service companies. The focus is on identifying and pulling the few massive levers that create immediate value, then deciding whether to exit, double down, or do dividend recaps.

### Strategic Philosophy

Hormozi emphasizes being "very selective" and not getting "eyes bigger than your stomach." The firm doesn't necessarily make more money by doing more deals

- success comes from finding the best fits, not just good deals. They're expanding their deal box by partnering with experienced operators who have scaled and exited $100M+ businesses.
