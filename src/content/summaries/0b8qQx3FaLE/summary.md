## Key Takeaways

Kova shares her professional creator workflow for achieving millions of views on Instagram Reels through a strategic combination of AI analysis, generation, and professional editing tools.

*   **Use AI agents like Manis to deconstruct successful content.** Provide a video link and ask it to analyze the **style, aesthetic keywords, script structure, and narrative flow** to create a detailed replication plan.

*   **Enhance production value with AI-generated elements.** Use tools like **Freepick** (with models like Nana Banana Pro for images, Cance or Cling for video) to add or modify backgrounds, objects, and create impossible transitions, transforming basic footage into visually rich scenes.

*   **Systematize creativity with templates and AI-assisted project management.** Maintain a structured workflow using **Obsidian** for notes and templates, and leverage **Claude Code** or **Cursor** to automate tasks like turning scripts into storyboards, ensuring consistency and scalability.

*   **Master a simple but powerful two-step editing enhancement.** For static shots, use AI to generate enhanced background elements and mask them in. For engaging hooks, use AI video generation between keyframes to create seamless, eye-catching transitions.

*   **Differentiation in the AI era comes from unique artistic application.** The competitive edge for creators is no longer just access to tools, but in developing a **distinct visual language and artistry** that AI helps execute efficiently.

*   **Prompt effectively by focusing on positive, descriptive language.** For video generation, describe the scene like a story, specifying camera action and avoiding negative terms (e.g., 'camera is static' vs. 'doesn't move'), and use prompt editors to optimize output.

## Summary

### Introduction and Core Philosophy

Greg Isenberg hosts creator Kova, known for her viral Instagram Reels with a distinctive 'cozy hacker' aesthetic and collaborations with major brands like Nvidia and Adobe. Kova argues that in today's fragmented creative landscape, **differentiation through artistry** is key, and AI tools are her primary means of achieving a high-production, unique visual style at scale. She asserts she "would not be a creator" without AI, framing it as a democratizing force that allows anyone to build Hollywood-level sets from their bedroom.

### Phase 1: Analysis and Planning with Manis AI

The workflow begins with **deconstructing successful content**. Kova demonstrates using **Manis AI**, an agentic tool that can actively browse and analyze videos. She prompts it with a link to one of her own videos and the request: "I really like the style and story of this video. Break it down in terms of style and aesthetic keywords... transcribe it and separate it into story sections... give me a plan for how I should replicate it."

Manis executes the task by opening the browser, watching the video, and producing a comprehensive breakdown. The analysis Kova receives is remarkably granular, correctly identifying:

*   **Overall Vibe:** "Dark academia maker, cozy hacker den bedroom devlog, personal tech nostalgia."
*   **Visual Language:** A blend of maker/hacker culture with kawaii nostalgia and cinematic bedroom aesthetics.

*   **Typography System:** Separation into titles, section headers, and captions.

*   **Narrative Structure:** A clean five-act 'build log' structure (Hook, Conflict & Stakes, The Build, Problem & Resolution, Tease/CTA).

*   **B-Roll Categories:** Close-up macro shots, screen recordings/process shots, and product reveal shots.

Manis then provides a **step-by-step replication plan**, advising on concept definition ('a one-sentence concept that is both technically interesting and emotionally resonant'), filming setup (dark room, RGB lighting, holding the project), and post-production checklist. Kova notes this is an excellent starting point for building a personal style guide by analyzing multiple favorite creators.

### Phase 2: Production Enhancement with AI Generation

Kova shifts to practical editing enhancements anyone can apply. Her first technique tackles **static talking-head shots**. Using a well-lit but simple frame, she imports a still into **Freepick**, specifically the **Nana Banana Pro** image model. Using the visual prompt feature, she simply annotates the background and writes general prompts like "more flowers" or "orangutan plush." She prefers simpler prompts to see what the model generates well, rather than over-specifying. The AI generates new background elements which she then masks into the original video in **Adobe Premiere Pro** (or CapCut), dramatically enriching the scene's visual interest and potential viewer retention.

Her second technique creates **dynamic transitions for video hooks**. To bridge two shots (e.g., a photo of herself as a child to her current self), she uses Freepick's video generator, favoring the **Cance** model. The key is **descriptive, positive prompting** framed as a story: "On the table is a picture of a kid. The kid in the center of the picture is waving their arms gleefully. The camera is static." She avoids negative language (e.g., "doesn't move") as models may latch onto the keyword. The generated 3-4 second clip is then stitched between the two shots, creating a seamless, engaging transition that boosts early video retention.

### Phase 3: Systematization and Project Management

To scale her content creation, Kova relies on systems built in **Obsidian** and augmented by AI coding assistants. She maintains a personal vault with custom templates, such as a **storyboard template** that breaks down videos shot-by-shot. She can then use **Claude Code** or **Cursor** (with the Claude model) to open her Obsidian vault and automate tasks. For example, she can command it to "turn my fruit script into the storyboard template," and the AI will parse her script and populate the template accordingly.

She also maintains a **'Kova Cut Style Guide'** within Obsidian. When starting a new project, she can ask Claude to do a first pass on a script or storyboard, following the rules and aesthetics defined in her guide. This creates a consistent, efficient pipeline from idea to execution, where AI handles the repetitive structuring work, allowing her to focus on creative direction.

### Toolstack and Final Thoughts

Kova concludes by summarizing her core toolkit:

*   **Video Editing:** Adobe Premiere Pro, After Effects.

*   **AI Image Generation:** Freepick (Nana Banana Pro model).

*   **AI Video Generation:** Freepick (Cance, Cling 3 models).

*   **Content Analysis:** Manis AI.

*   **Project Planning:** Obsidian, augmented by Claude Code or Cursor.

*   **Task Management:** Poke.

Her final advice is that there is "no better time than now to make art," emphasizing that the tools for professional-grade, distinctive storytelling are accessible to all. The future belongs to creators who strategically wield these tools to develop and execute their unique artistic vision.
