## Summary

The video explains the fundamental negotiation principle of **BATNA (Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement)**, a concept from Harvard Business School. Research from institutions like London Business School shows that negotiators who know their alternatives set higher goals, make more aggressive opening offers, and ultimately secure better deals. Your BATNA acts as a psychological anchor and a source of power, creating a decision standard where you only accept terms that are superior to your best alternative.

### Building Your BATNA

You must establish your BATNA **before** sitting down at the negotiation table. This involves actively creating or identifying your best fallback option.

- **For Employees**: Get another job offer before negotiating with your current employer for a raise or promotion.

- **For Business Purchases/Vendors**: Secure multiple bids or offers to compare terms and create competition.

- **For Selling**: Negotiate from the position of not needing to sell (e.g., your business is profitable and can continue operating).

- **General Principle**: You want to buy when you don't *need* to buy, and sell when you don't *need* to sell, because you have a viable alternative.

### The Power of a Strong BATNA

A strong BATNA transforms your negotiation posture. If you have other customers lined up or a profitable business to fall back on, you can confidently ask for significantly better terms (e.g., $11 instead of $10). Without a BATNA, you are forced to rely on bluffing tactics, which is likened to "trying to win at poker only on bluffing"—a weak position.

You can use your BATNA overtly by presenting a competing offer and asking the other party to beat it, or you can keep it as a private source of confidence that allows you to aim higher. The speaker illustrates this with personal examples, including negotiating to buy a home while being perfectly happy with his current house, and selling his companies (Gym Launch, Prestige Labs) from the position of not needing the sale.
