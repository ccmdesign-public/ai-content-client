## Key Takeaways

This video advocates for a modern approach to wireframing using AI to build functional prototypes in code, not static mockups.

*   **Replace static wireframes with functional prototypes** built directly in your codebase using AI tools, enabling real click-through testing in minutes instead of weeks.

*   **Use AI's 'planning mode' as a collaborative partner** to ask critical questions about user flow, components, and logic before writing any code, ensuring a solid foundation.

*   **Maintain the 'wireframe mindset' for early validation** by keeping prototypes low-fidelity to focus feedback on structure and flow, not visual polish, preventing 'AI-built' products that feel off.

*   **Iterate seamlessly from wireframe to final UI** since the prototype lives in the codebase; you can refine UX, test alternatives (like chat-based vs. traditional flows), and build the final product on the same foundation.

## Summary

The video argues that the traditional product design process is broken because AI has made it too easy to jump straight to high-fidelity prototypes, skipping the crucial wireframing stage where deep UX thinking happens. This results in products that 'feel off' because they lack validated structure and flow.

### The Modern Wireframing Solution

Instead of creating static sketches in Figma, the presenter demonstrates building a **functional, clickable wireframe prototype directly in the codebase in under 20 minutes**. Using an AI-assisted development environment, she starts with a user flow diagram for a Cal.com onboarding feature. The AI, prompted to act as a 'world-class product designer,' generates the wireframe code using existing component libraries.

### The Critical 'Planning Mode' Step

A key part of the process is using the AI's **'planning mode,'** which acts like a co-founder or CTO. It asks clarifying questions about visual style, navigation logic, and component usage *before* generating any code. This ensures the team aligns on foundational decisions and the AI builds the correct structure from the onset.

### Iteration and Validation

Once the basic wireframe is built, the team can immediately click through it to validate the flow. The presenter identifies issues (e.g., too many steps, unclear logic) and iterates rapidly. She even prompts the AI to generate an **alternative, chat-based onboarding flow** for comparison, creating two testable concepts in a fraction of the traditional time.

### From Wireframe to Final Product

Because the wireframe is built with real code in the project's branch, it becomes the **foundation for the final product**. Teams can iterate on the UX, apply the full UI design system, and refine logic without starting from scratch. This streamlines the entire product process, compressing a workflow that traditionally took weeks into a single afternoon.

## Context

This video addresses a critical gap in modern, AI-accelerated product development. As teams use AI to generate polished prototypes instantly, they often bypass the low-fidelity wireframing stage essential for validating core user experience (UX) and information architecture. This leads to products that look finished but have fundamental usability flaws. The demonstrated method is crucial for product managers, designers, and developers in SaaS and B2B companies who need to ship high-quality products faster without sacrificing thoughtful UX design. It connects to broader trends in AI-assisted development and the need for human oversight in the AI product lifecycle.
