## Key Takeaways

Recent demonstrations reveal rapid advancements in Chinese humanoid robotics, blending technical capability with strategic industrial marketing. • **Whole-body dynamic coordination** enables complex physical tasks like sword dances and aerial flips through real-time AI control systems. • **Public stress testing** at major events like China's Spring Festival Gala serves as industrial marketing and validation for factory deployment. • **Mass production scaling** is now the focus, with companies like Unitry aiming for 20,000 shipments in 2026 and Agibbot targeting tens of thousands.

## Summary

### Rooter's L7 Sword Dance Demonstration

Rooter's L7 humanoid robot performed a full traditional sword dance to mark Chinese New Year, serving as a technical demonstration of advanced robotics. The 5.6-foot tall robot uses a titanium and carbon fiber frame with 55 independently actuated joints, allowing for complex whole-body movements. Its embedded AI system handles **real-time motion planning**, torque control, and dynamic coordination to execute fast directional changes, jumps, and spins while avoiding self-injury with the sword.

### Unitry's G1 and H1 Robots at Spring Festival Gala

During China's nationally televised Spring Festival Gala, Unitry deployed fleets of G1 and H1 robots performing drunken kung fu, parkour, and 3-meter aerial flips alongside human performers. The robots demonstrated **multi-robot coordination** while moving at 4 m/s, with one robot executing a spin-based recovery after a fall. The event represented strategic industrial marketing, with reports of 100 million yuan invested in production connected to the show.

### Agibbot's Expedition A3 and Live Performances

Agibbot showcased its Expedition A3 robot executing high-difficulty kung fu maneuvers including aerial flying kicks and mid-air steps without CGI. Designed for retail assistance and entertainment, it features **anthropomorphic degrees of freedom**, a flexible waist, and an end-to-end large AI model for natural interaction. The company hosted a 60-minute robot-led gala in Shanghai featuring 16 humanoids performing synchronized routines as a live stress test.

### International Context and Production Scaling

• **Unitry's CEO** stated the company aims to ship 20,000 humanoid robots in 2026, nearly four times their 2025 shipments. • **Agibbot** projects shipments exceeding 5,100 units by end of 2025 with mass production planned for 2026. • **UK startup Humanoid** claims record development speed, transitioning from drawings to a bipedal robot in just 3 months using a global development approach. • **Boston Dynamics Spot** is already deployed in a 1.5 million square foot US aerospace factory for autonomous inspection and digital twin creation.

## Context

This matters because humanoid robotics is transitioning from research demonstrations to industrial deployment at scale. Chinese companies are using high-profile cultural performances to showcase technical capabilities while signaling serious manufacturing ambitions. The competition with Western counterparts like Tesla's Optimus is intensifying, with Chinese firms demonstrating both advanced agility and concrete production plans. Businesses in manufacturing, logistics, and service industries should monitor these developments as humanoid robots move toward practical workplace applications.
