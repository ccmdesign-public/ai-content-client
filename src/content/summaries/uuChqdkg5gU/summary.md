## Summary

The video begins by establishing the critical importance of appearing in AI search results from platforms like ChatGPT, Google's AI Overviews, and Perplexity. These tools shape buyer decisions before they reach a website, making visibility at this stage strategically vital. ### The Manual Research Method

The first approach involves manually analyzing AI search results for your target product/service queries. For example, a skincare brand selling cleansers would search terms like "good skin cleanser" or "best skin cleansers for acne." The goal is twofold:

*   **Identify if your brand type is citable:** For some queries, AI tools may only cite third-party media or review sites, not brand-owned content. If that's the case, a digital PR strategy to get featured in those third-party sites may be more effective than creating your own content.

*   **Analyze winning content structure:** When a brand's page *is* cited, examine it to understand the subtopics it covers. AI tools often break a main query into multiple sub-queries; your content must comprehensively answer all of them to be cited.

This manual process is time-consuming, as you must repeat it across many searches and track results consistently.

### The Faster, Tool-Based Method

The video demonstrates using **Semrush's AI visibility toolkit** to drastically speed up this research. The process involves:
1.  Entering your domain (e.g., dove.com) to see your current AI search visibility score and performance across different AI platforms.

This tool automatically aggregates and categorizes opportunities, bypassing the need for manual, repetitive searches.

### Prioritizing and Implementing Findings

With a list of potential topics, the presenter recommends applying filters:
1.  **Relevance:** Remove topics completely unrelated to your products/services.
2.  **Commercial Intent:** Focus on queries implying purchase intent (e.g., "good face cleanser for dry skin") over purely informational ones (e.g., "what does face cleanser mean").
3.  **Content Audit:** Check if you already have content on a target topic that can be improved or expanded.

Finally, the video stresses that AI tools favor **pillar-based content clusters**—a main hub page covering a broad topic (e.g., "Sensitive Skin Care") linked to detailed articles on subtopics (e.g., "Dry Sensitive Skin," "Oily Sensitive Skin")—over a disorganized blog with scattered articles.

## Context

As AI search platforms like ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, and Perplexity become primary sources for consumer research, they are intercepting potential customers before they visit traditional websites. This shift means brands risk losing visibility at the most critical, decision-making stage of the buyer's journey if they are not cited in these AI-generated answers. Marketing leaders, content strategists, and SEO professionals must adapt their content strategies beyond traditional SEO to capture this new traffic source, which often drives highly qualified, purchase-ready visitors.
