## Key Takeaways

The core advice for finding a long-term partner focuses on fundamental self-improvement and clear communication. Key takeaways include:

*   **Get in shape:** This is the easiest it will ever be and removes a major physical constraint, making you more attractive to a wider pool of people.

*   **Get rich (or signal ambition):** Wealth signals the ability to gain resources, but ambition and future potential (like a "starving artist" with upside) can be equally or more attractive than existing wealth.

*   **Don't be a dick:** This means avoiding behaviors that decrease the likelihood a woman wants to continue a relationship with you, and being upfront about your non-negotiables early on.

*   **Focus on unchanging core behaviors:** Instead of matching on fleeting interests, identify and align on the few foundational behaviors and preferences that are unlikely to change over decades.

## Summary

### The Three Foundational Principles

Hormozi presents a straightforward three-step framework for men seeking a high-quality, long-term partner. First, **get in shape**. He argues this is a non-negotiable baseline that dramatically expands your options, as nearly everyone at a healthy weight is physically attractive. He uses a confrontational James Clear-inspired tactic, telling a busy employee that if they can't prioritize fitness now, they should accept they never will.

Second, **get rich**. He clarifies that it's less about having money and more about **signaling the ability to gain resources**. A trust fund kid may have wealth, but a "young, hungry guy" or a "starving artist" with clear upside potential often signals more ambition and future success, which is highly attractive.

Third, **don't be a dick**. This is a bucket term for behaviors that make someone not want to be around you. The key is to understand the preferences of your target partner and avoid negatives that are non-costly for you to change.

### Practical Dating and Relationship Strategy

A major theme is **radical upfront honesty**. Hormozi recommends stating your non-negotiables (e.g., "I work a lot," "I work out") on the first or second date to avoid building a relationship on a false persona. The goal is to minimize the gap between your "dating performance" and your true baseline self.

He advises focusing on **what won't change** over a 50-year horizon, rather than current shared hobbies. Identify the few "big rock" core behaviors and values (like how you handle stress or communicate) that are predictive of long-term compatibility, and ignore the "hundreds of pebbles" on a superficial checklist.

### Lessons from a Successful Marriage

Hormozi shares insights from his marriage to Leila. They proactively took a **divorce course** to understand and preemptively address common divorce reasons like money, kids, and communication. Their relationship thrives on a **low-emotion, behavioral feedback loop**.

Leila's "superpower" is being **hyper-responsive to feedback**, changing behavior permanently after a single request, provided it doesn't incur a significant personal cost. This requires open communication about preferences (e.g., needing space after work) without extrapolating to dramatic conclusions (e.g., "you don't love me").

He highlights the power of **specific appreciation**. For him, "Thank you for working so hard" or "Thank you for dinner" is a more meaningful reinforcer than generic praise like "I'm proud of you," as it acknowledges deliberate effort and service to the partnership.
