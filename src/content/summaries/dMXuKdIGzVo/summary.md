## Key Takeaways

Anthropic has significantly enhanced Claude's integration with Microsoft Office, moving beyond basic chat to programmable automation.

*   **Skills are now available in Excel and PowerPoint**, letting you import custom, repeatable AI workflows (like auditing slide decks or analyzing financial models) as slash commands.

*   **You can share Skills across your entire organization**, enabling teams to standardize processes like board presentation formatting or financial variance analysis.

*   **The Claude extension provides real-time, contextual awareness** of your open Office files, allowing for seamless editing and feedback between the co-work interface and the desktop apps.

## Summary

Anthropic has released a major update for Claude, deeply integrating its **Skills** functionality into Microsoft Excel and PowerPoint. Previously, the Claude extension for Office provided contextual feedback, but now users can import and execute custom-built **Skills**—repeatable AI workflows—directly within these applications via slash commands.

### PowerPoint Integration

The demo shows a custom Skill that makes a slide deck "board-ready" by auditing every slide against specific standards. For example, it finds slides with three or more bullet points and rewrites them as a single bold headline with a short supporting line. The Skill can also enforce brand colors (via hex codes), preferred wording, and avoid AI-sounding phrases. Users access Skills through a new "Manage Skills" panel in the extension, where they can upload, toggle, and configure them.

### Excel Integration

The update brings financial modeling-specific Skills to Excel, with out-of-the-box slash commands like `/audit`, `/cleandata`, `/dcfmodel`, and `/compsanalysis`. The video demonstrates uploading a custom "variance analysis" Skill that audits an Excel file for formula errors, calculates actual vs. target variances, and adds plain-English explanations as cell comments—all executed within a minute.

### Enhanced Co-Work & Monitoring

The Claude extension now has a setting that allows it to **work across files and maintain real-time awareness** of open Office sessions. This creates interoperability where Claude Co-work can monitor changes made via the extension and vice versa, enabling a continuous feedback loop. Users can also add custom instructions to the extension, creating a pseudo-system prompt that compounds with the power of imported Skills.

## Context

This update matters because it moves AI assistance in productivity software from general-purpose chat to specialized, repeatable automation. Professionals in finance, consulting, venture capital, and any role reliant on Excel models or PowerPoint presentations can now encode their company's specific workflows and quality standards into AI Skills. This allows for deterministic, high-quality outputs at scale and facilitates knowledge sharing across teams, significantly boosting efficiency and consistency in daily office tasks.
