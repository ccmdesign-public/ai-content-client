Building content live with your community can be more valuable than polished recordings. The primary value of a community is often the interaction and peer support, not just the formal content. ## Summary

The video directly addresses a common founder's dilemma: the fear of launching an incomplete product. The advice is to launch your community business now, even with minimal or no pre-built course content. The speaker emphasizes that waiting to build the 'perfect' offer in isolation is counterproductive because you need market feedback to know what to build.

A powerful example is given: the recent winner of the speaker's 'school games' launched with nothing in their classroom. This validates the strategy of starting with just a promise and building the content live with the community. This live, interactive creation process is framed as a potential value-add, as members can have their questions answered in real-time during content creation.

The speaker challenges the assumed necessity of a formal 'classroom' module altogether. Data from their platform shows that the vast majority of user activity and value comes from the community feed—people posting, commenting, and interacting—not from consuming static course recordings. Therefore, the primary focus should be on facilitating conversation and peer support around the topic.

The actionable takeaway is to stop guessing what the market wants and start finding out by asking them directly within the launched community. A simple but effective tactic is shared: directly ask members to engage. Often, people need explicit permission and encouragement to overcome the fear of looking foolish. The founder's role is to ask questions, prompt discussions, and foster a safe space for interaction from day one.

## Context

This advice is crucial for entrepreneurs, creators, and anyone building a community-based business or membership site. It counters the pervasive 'build it and they will come' mentality that leads to wasted time and misaligned products. In the current landscape of online education and digital communities, engagement and network effects are often more valuable than static content libraries. This mindset shift towards rapid validation and customer-centric development is fundamental to modern startup and content creation methodologies.
