## Key Takeaways

The video explains how to structure a small core team across multiple businesses by focusing on essential functions rather than rigid roles.

## Summary

Alex Hormozi discusses structuring a small core team across a portfolio of businesses generating $20 million in revenue. He challenges the premise of needing three specific 'people' and instead advises focusing on three essential **functions**: **Advertising/Conversion** (getting customers), **Product/Service Delivery** (building and maintaining the offering), and **Operations/Team Leadership** (running day-to-day, building culture, recruiting).

He emphasizes that these functions must occur, whether handled by one person or many. The key is to identify your business's primary **constraint**: are you **demand constrained** (need more customers) or **supply constrained** (can't deliver enough product/service)?

Hiring should directly address the current constraint. If demand-constrained but no one can advertise, hire for that function. If not demand-constrained, ignore advertising until it becomes the bottleneck. Growth follows a rhythm of alternating between fixing demand and then supply, never tackling both sides simultaneously.

## Context

This advice is crucial for entrepreneurs, startup founders, and small business operators managing multiple ventures or a holding company structure. It addresses the common challenge of resource allocation and prioritization in a lean team, providing a framework to scale efficiently by focusing only on the immediate bottleneck.
