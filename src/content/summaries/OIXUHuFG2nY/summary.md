## Key Takeaways

The video advises entrepreneurs to avoid wasting time seeking external advice on decisions that can be resolved through data or experimentation. Key insights include: • **Don't ask math questions** - Use spreadsheets to calculate metrics like **LTV to CAC ratio** for allocation decisions • **Don't ask testable questions** - Run small-scale experiments (like A/B testing book titles) to gather real-world data • **Test before scaling** - Use low-cost market tests ($1,000 promotions) to validate expansion opportunities before major investments

## Summary

The video identifies two categories of questions entrepreneurs should avoid asking others because they can be answered through systematic approaches.

**Questions Solvable with Math** represent about 30% of common business questions. These are quantitative decisions that can be resolved with basic calculations. For example, when deciding which product to focus on, entrepreneurs should calculate the **lifetime value (LTV)** and **customer acquisition cost (CAC)** for each option, then allocate resources to the product with the higher LTV to CAC ratio. This eliminates subjective opinions and provides clear, data-driven answers.

**Questions Requiring Testing** involve uncertainties about customer preferences or market viability. The speaker shares a personal example of testing book titles for "The Leads Book" by running variations like "$100 Million Promotions," "$100 Million Advertising," and "$100 Million Marketing" to see which resonated most. Through systematic testing of titles, covers, and subheadlines, they discovered customers wanted "leads" rather than "advertising" - similar to people wanting holes in walls rather than drills.

**Practical Testing Framework** provides a concrete method for market expansion decisions. For brick-and-mortar businesses considering new markets: take $1,000, run your best promotion in five potential markets, measure lead costs in each, then expand into the market with the lowest lead cost. This approach prevents wasting $250,000 on extensive studies and physical build-outs before validating market viability.

The video concludes by promoting a free **10-stage roadmap** for scaling businesses from zero to $100 million+, covering software, physical products, service businesses, and brick-and-mortar operations across eight business functions.

## Context

This advice addresses a common problem in entrepreneurship: decision paralysis and over-reliance on external opinions. Many business owners waste time and resources seeking advice on questions they could answer themselves with basic data analysis or small experiments. The guidance is particularly relevant for founders, business operators, and marketers making allocation decisions or testing new markets. In an era of data accessibility, this approach emphasizes empirical validation over guesswork, aligning with lean startup methodologies and data-driven business practices.
