## Key Takeaways

Anthropic's recent updates transform Claude from a chat tool into a persistent, always-on assistant that can operate your computer and manage complex workflows. Key features include: **Computer Use** (Claude controls mouse/keyboard to use any app), **Dispatch** (orchestrate tasks from your phone), **Remote Control** (mobile access to Claude Code), and **Channels** (event-driven automation via Telegram/Discord).

## Summary

Over the past month, Anthropic has aggressively responded to the competitive landscape by shipping a "boatload" of features for Claude, fundamentally shifting its interaction model from a tool you operate to an assistant you delegate to.

### Computer Use: The Ultimate Primitive

The most significant announcement allows **Claude to control your computer**—using mouse, keyboard, and screen to operate any application. This isn't simulation; it's real system control. Claude can open files, navigate browsers, edit spreadsheets, and batch process photos in Photoshop. Combined with **Dispatch**, you can assign tasks from your phone and have Claude execute them on your desktop while you're away. This capability is especially valuable for legacy software without modern APIs.

### New Interaction Modalities

Anthropic introduced multiple ways to interact with Claude remotely:

*   **Dispatch**: A persistent conversation thread that runs on your computer. Message it from your phone to spawn and manage multiple **Claude Co-work** or **Claude Code** sessions. It acts as an orchestrator, not just a chat interface.

*   **Remote Control**: Access your local **Claude Code** terminal session from your mobile device. Work starts on your machine, and you can pick it up via a QR code or URL on your phone, with full access to your local file system and tools.

*   **Channels**: **MCP servers** that push events (like CI failures, webhooks, or chat messages) into your running **Claude Code** session. This enables hackable, event-driven automation via platforms like **Telegram** and **Discord**.

*   **Scheduled Tasks**: Set recurring tasks in both **Claude Co-work** and **Claude Code**, either locally or via cloud infrastructure, for automated workflows like morning briefings or error log analysis.

### A Shift in Mental Model

The collective impact of these features represents a **relationship shift**. Users stop thinking of Claude as a tool and start treating it as something they delegate to and check in with. As one user noted, it changes how you structure your day, allowing work to run in parallel while you're elsewhere. The goal is an **always-on, context-maintaining, persistent interactive orchestration experience**.

### Additional Upgrades

Beyond the flagship features, Anthropic also released:

*   **Projects** in Claude Co-work for better organization.

*   **Code Review** with a team of agents in Claude Code.

*   General availability of the **1 million token context window** for Claude Opus and Sonnet.

*   Interactive charts/diagrams in the main Claude app.

*   Integrated skills and context sharing between **Claude for Excel** and **Claude for PowerPoint**.

*   Free tier access to memory and connectors.

## Context

This wave of updates is part of the 'Clawification' trend, where AI products are adopting features popularized by OpenClaw (like persistent agents and remote control). It represents a strategic move by Anthropic to position Claude not just as a language model, but as a comprehensive agentic platform capable of automating real work. This matters for developers, knowledge workers, and businesses seeking to leverage AI for complex, multi-application workflows and increased productivity.
