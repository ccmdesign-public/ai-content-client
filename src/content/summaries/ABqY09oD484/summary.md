## Key Takeaways

The core advice for developers is to adopt AI assistants with persistence.

*   The biggest barrier is not giving AI coding tools a **genuine chance** to integrate into your workflow.

*   You must **keep trying** because models improve rapidly; a feature that failed 3 months ago may work perfectly today.

*   The value is a **cultural shift** towards using AI, not just adopting a single new feature.

## Summary

The key to leveling up as a developer is embracing a cultural shift towards using AI coding assistants, with persistence being the most critical factor. The biggest limitation for developers is simply not giving these tools a sustained chance to prove their worth.

Many developers who tried AI assistants like GitHub Copilot early on were rightfully frustrated when the tools couldn't perform basic tasks, such as writing simple HTML. This early disappointment, compounded by premature hype calling it "the future" before it was fully realized, created a barrier to adoption.

However, the pace of advancement in AI models and their integration harnesses is now so rapid that capabilities change dramatically in just months. A task that was impossible or poorly executed three months ago can be handled seamlessly by the latest models today, such as Opus 46. This means a developer's past negative experience is no longer a reliable indicator of current potential.

The actionable advice is to adopt a mindset of continuous experimentation. If you tried an AI assistant and found it frustrating, you were correct *then*, but you should try it again *now*. The goal is to integrate trying these tools into your regular development rhythm, persistently testing them until they effectively work for your specific tasks and workflow.

## Context

This advice is critical as AI coding assistants transition from novel gimmicks to essential productivity tools in software development. Developers who dismiss these tools based on outdated experiences risk falling behind in efficiency and capability. The discussion reflects a broader industry trend where the rapid iteration of AI models is fundamentally changing development workflows, making adaptability and a willingness to re-evaluate tools a key career skill.
