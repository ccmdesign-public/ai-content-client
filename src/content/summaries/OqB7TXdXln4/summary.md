## Key Takeaways

The video reveals the collaborative second-screen features in major AI chatbots that most users overlook. Key insights include: • **Canvas/Artifacts** are dedicated workspaces in ChatGPT/Gemini (Canvas) and Claude (Artifacts) for building, not just chatting. • **Three primary use cases** deliver the most value: collaborative writing with targeted edits, creating compelling visuals/diagrams, and building ephemeral micro-tools. • **Platform strengths differ**: ChatGPT excels at writing/editing workflows, Gemini/Claude are best for visuals, and Claude/ChatGPT allow sharing created tools.

## Summary

Major AI chatbots contain a dedicated workspace feature adjacent to the chat interface that enables collaborative creation. This feature is called **Canvas** in ChatGPT and Gemini, and **Artifacts** in Claude. Most users either never open it or use it unintentionally, but it's where AI transitions from talking to building.

**Activating the Feature**
In ChatGPT and Gemini, you can explicitly toggle Canvas on via a setting (plus sign under "More" in ChatGPT, settings cog in Gemini). For Claude, you must explicitly prompt the AI to "use the artifacts feature." You can also prompt ChatGPT/Gemini to "use the canvas feature."

**Three High-Value Use Cases**
1.  **Collaborative Writing & Targeted Editing**: This solves the "rewriting trap" where AI rewrites an entire document when you only want a section edited, wasting time and context window. Using Canvas/Artifacts allows for **targeted edits** where you highlight a specific paragraph and instruct the AI to change only that. ChatGPT and Gemini excel here with built-in features like version history, suggested edits, and reading level/tone adjustments.
2.  **Creating Compelling Visuals**: This is highlighted as the most overlooked yet highest-value application. You can turn meeting transcripts or SOPs into flow diagrams, infographics, or animated data visualizations for training materials or presentations. Gemini can create infographics and web pages from transcripts, while Claude can generate animated data visualizations you can screen-record for presentations.
3.  **Building Ephemeral Micro-Tools**: These are simple, temporary tools for specific scenarios, like a commission calculator for a sales team. You build, use, and discard them. Claude and ChatGPT allow you to share these tools via a public link, while Gemini does not easily support sharing.

**Platform Recommendations & Rules of Thumb**
• **Writing/Editing**: Use **ChatGPT** for best results (comment features, targeted edits). Gemini is okay, Claude is weak.
• **Visuals**: Use **Gemini** or **Claude** for creating diagrams and infographics.
• **Tools**: Use **Claude** or **ChatGPT** for building shareable micro-tools.

Reach for Canvas/Artifacts when: (1) the output needs multiple rounds of refinement/edits, or (2) the output is visual and needs to be seen or shared with others.

## Context

This matters because most users interact with AI chatbots only through the chat window, missing powerful built-in features for co-creation. Professionals, teams, and content creators can significantly boost productivity by using these workspaces for iterative editing, visual asset generation, and rapid prototyping of simple tools. It reflects the broader trend of AI evolving from a conversational partner to a collaborative builder within enterprise and creative workflows.
