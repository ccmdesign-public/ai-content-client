## Key Takeaways

OpenAI's GPT 5.4 represents a significant leap in AI capabilities with practical applications. Key highlights include:

- **Massive context and computer use**: Supports up to 1 million tokens with state-of-the-art browser/computer interaction capabilities

- **Superior coding and reasoning**: Combines GPT 5.3 Codex's coding strengths with improved reasoning and agentic workflows

- **Real-world knowledge work**: Delivers more polished results on practical tasks like document generation and presentations

- **Competitive but expensive**: Outperforms Anthropic's Opus 4.6 across benchmarks but costs $30/$180 per million tokens for input/output

## Summary

OpenAI has released GPT 5.4 with two model variants: GPT 5.4 Thinking available in ChatGPT Plus/Teams/Pro/Enterprise, and GPT 5.4 available in the $200/month tier and via API. The model introduces several breakthrough capabilities that make it a versatile workhorse for various applications.

### Core Capabilities

**Extended Context and Computer Use**: GPT 5.4 supports up to 1 million tokens of context, with state-of-the-art computer use capabilities. This enables complex browser-based automation tasks like email sorting, subscription management, and content updates. The model can provide upfront plans of its thinking process and allows mid-course adjustments during responses, similar to Anthropic's interleaf thinking approach.

**Enhanced Reasoning and Coding**: Building on GPT 5.2's general reasoning, GPT 5.4 delivers more consistent and polished results on real-world knowledge work. It combines the coding strengths of GPT 5.3 Codex with leading computer use capabilities, making it particularly effective for front-end development tasks. Examples include building complex web applications like theme park simulations and RPG games with interactive 3D environments.

### Performance Benchmarks

The model shows significant improvements across multiple benchmarks:

- **OS World**: Surpasses human performance at 75% vs 72.4%
- **BrowseComp and WebArena**: Considerable improvements in browser-based tasks

- **Medium Reasoning**: Particularly strong performance where GPT 5.3 Codex struggled

- **Token Efficiency**: Most token-efficient model yet, potentially reducing costs despite higher per-token pricing

### Practical Applications

**Document and Presentation Generation**: GPT 5.4 produces more professional and cohesive outputs compared to previous models, with better design coherence, proper table formatting, and overall polish that resembles human-created work.

**Agentic Workflows**: The model excels at delegatable browser tasks, making it suitable for automating repetitive web-based work. Developers report it's more natural and assertive, working through ambiguous problems without second-guessing and proactively parallelizing work.

### Pricing and Availability

**Cost Structure**: Input costs $30 per million tokens, output costs $180 per million tokens. Requests exceeding 272,000 tokens of context are charged at 2x the normal rate for exceeded tokens.

**Competitive Positioning**: While more expensive than Anthropic's Opus 4.6 ($5/$25 per million tokens) and Sonnet 4.6 ($3/$15 per million tokens), GPT 5.4 outperforms them across most benchmarks. The model also features a new fast mode in Codex that runs at 1.5x speed with the same intelligence.

## Context

GPT 5.4 represents a significant step in AI's evolution toward practical, real-world applications. This release matters because it moves beyond theoretical benchmarks to deliver tangible improvements in knowledge work, coding, and computer automation. Developers, product teams, and businesses looking to automate complex workflows should pay attention, as these capabilities enable new levels of productivity and automation. The model's performance surpassing humans on certain benchmarks signals AI's growing competence at tasks previously requiring human intelligence.
