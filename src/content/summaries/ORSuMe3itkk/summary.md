## Key Takeaways

Hormozi's advice for scaling a profitable, in-person business training company focuses on fixing the acquisition bottleneck.

- **Standardize the Sales Pitch:** Create a master slide deck with every word scripted (1,700+ slides, one sentence per slide) to eliminate variability and reduce speaker training from months to a week.

- **Double Down on Your Strengths:** If you have a profitable, in-person model (50%+ margins), scale that instead of pivoting to a new, untested channel like online marketing.

- **Train with Repetition, Not Supervision:** New speakers should rehearse the standardized pitch 25-50 times independently via recording before ever pitching live, saving founder time and minimizing risk.

## Summary

The video features Alex Hormozi advising Adam, a business training consultant whose company generated £14.8M last year and aims to scale to £25M. Adam's primary constraint is scaling his acquisition method, which relies on 12-15 free, in-person monthly events where speakers pitch a £1,000 offer, converting about 250 attendees per month.

Hormozi's core solution is to **standardize the pitch** completely. He recommends creating a master slide deck with **1,700+ slides**, where each slide contains **one sentence or phrase** of a word-for-word script, paired with a supporting visual. This method, which Hormozi used for a 90-minute presentation, ensures consistency, maximizes audience comprehension, and keeps engagement high by changing slides every 6-10 seconds.

**The training process for new speakers is revolutionized.** Instead of the founder spending months personally coaching each speaker, the standardized deck allows training to focus solely on delivery: pace, volume, and hand gestures. New hires should practice the 90-minute pitch **25-50 times on their own**, recording themselves each time. The founder then reviews the best recording at 2x speed, provides minimal feedback, and only allows a live pitch once proficiency is proven. This slashes the founder's time investment and dramatically lowers the risk of a botched, costly presentation.

Hormozi strongly advises **against changing the business model**. Given the company's proven profitability (~50% margins) and electric performance in-person, the smarter path is to remove the acquisition bottleneck by perfecting and scaling the existing event model, rather than competing in a new, harder arena like online marketing.

The video concludes with Hormozi offering a free **10-stage roadmap** for scaling a business from zero to $100M+, based on his experience across software, service, and brick-and-mortar companies, available at `acquisition.com/roadmap`.
