## Summary

### Introduction and Business Potential

Chris Koerner introduces the concept of a highly profitable, overlooked business: parking lot striping and seal coating. He meets with Hunter, who started with minimal investment and achieved $46,825 in revenue in his second month. The core premise is that this is a **visual, scalable service** with significant demand, low startup costs, and the potential for daily revenue reaching $13,000 on large seal coating jobs. Hunter was inspired by a previous podcast guest and liked the business because it required little money to start, the work was tangible, and it could grow from small $200 jobs to $20,000+ projects quickly.

### Getting Started: From Zero to First Dollar

Hunter's journey began with practicing on an old parking lot using just water in his machine to get a feel for the process. His first actual job was a free, five-line project for a nonprofit, costing only $5 in materials. This served as a learning experience and portfolio builder. For his **first paying customer**, he used a clever Facebook strategy: he posted in his town's local page, asking the community to vote for which business should get a new parking lot. This generated engagement, got his name out, and allowed him to reach out to all nominated businesses to see if they would pay for the service. His second job, secured via cold email, was a massive jump to 250 lines for a church, priced at $1,190.

### Sales, Pricing, and Explosive Growth

Hunter's primary customer acquisition method shifted to **cold email outreach**. He hired someone on Upwork to scrape lead lists (businesses, particularly churches) for $5-$6 per list. His initial emails positioned him as new and offering good prices. He found a **close rate of 40-50%**, which improved when he quoted jobs in person. His first month revenue was just over $2,800 with about 75% profit as he did all the work himself. The second month exploded to $46,825 in revenue as quotes from the first month converted. He took home just under $20,000 in profit that month after accounting for new equipment and subcontracting some work. He emphasizes **professionalism over appearances**вЂ”he landed a $26,000 job while showing up in a beat-up minivan.

### Service Breakdown: Striping, Crack Filling, and Seal Coating

The interview provides a detailed on-site breakdown of the three core services. For **line striping**, Hunter prices at **$5-$7 per linear foot** for standard lines, with upcharges for complex layouts, arrows, and stencils (e.g., $10-$25 for handicap symbols). A sample 82-line church lot was quoted at $760, taking 2-2.5 hours with ~$130 in material cost, yielding over $250/hour profit. **Crack filling** is priced by the linear foot (30 cents to $1). He notes the work is fast but material costs can vary greatly with crack depth. **Seal coating** is the most lucrative; it's priced by the square foot (15-25 cents). A 46,900 sq ft lot would be a $9,000+ job, with material costs around 6 cents/sq ft and labor for a crew being a small percentage, leading to high net profit margins, even when the work is subcontracted.

### Equipment, Logistics, and Final Advice

Hunter started with a **used Graco line striper** bought for $2,300 (new is $6,000-$7,000). He recommends **PPG Flint Fast Dry paint** (~$100/5 gallons) over more expensive Sherwin-Williams options. For crack filling, he bought a new machine for $1,500, and for seal coating, a used trailer with tank and pump for $6,000. He stresses that one can **start with just a $10 paint roller and a bucket** to build a portfolio and save for equipment. His final advice is to **just go for it**, as it's low-risk with high demand. He suggests owning the fact you're new in marketing and using local Facebook groups to find initial small jobs that larger companies won't take.
