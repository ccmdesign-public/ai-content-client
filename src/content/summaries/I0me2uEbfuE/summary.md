## Summary

The video serves as a guide to the major AI models available in 2026, explaining their capabilities, pricing tiers, and ideal use cases.

**ChatGPT** from OpenAI is highlighted as a versatile, consumer-friendly general model. It excels in ease of use and offers features like image generation, PDF ingestion, and voice mode. Pricing tiers include a free plan, an $8/month Go plan, a Plus plan with advanced reasoning models, and a $200/month Pro plan offering unlimited access to flagship models.

**Claude** from Anthropic is presented as a top contender for work tasks and coding, though it lacks image generation. It is praised for its strong integrations with tools like Gmail, Notion, and Slack, and its "skills" feature for customizing outputs. Plans range from a generous free tier to Pro ($17/$20/month) and Max ($100/month) tiers, offering more usage and specialized features like Claude for Excel and PowerPoint.

**Gemini** from Google is noted for its speed, large context window, and unique ability to ingest and analyze video. It also boasts the best integrations with Google products (Gmail, Drive) and superior web search capabilities. The presenter uses Gemini for deep research. Its free plan offers access to multiple models, with paid tiers (Plus, Pro, Ultra) providing increased quotas and access to video models.

**Grok** from xAI is described as a specialist model, incredibly effective for searching and analyzing live Twitter/X data for trends and research, but not as broadly capable as the other frontier models. Pricing includes free and paid tiers up to $300/month.

**Open-Source Models** are covered for users seeking privacy, control, and local operation. While requiring more technical setup and being less powerful than frontier models, they are sufficient for 95% of use cases. Key examples include Meta's Llama, DeepSeek, OpenAI's GPTOSS, Nvidia's Neotron, and Google's Gemma.

**Specialized Models** are also explored. **Image Generation** models like Midjourney, DALL-E (ChatGPT Image), Stable Diffusion, and Google's Nano Banana are discussed, with some being open-source and runnable locally. **Video Generation** models include OpenAI's Sora 2, Google's VO3, Runway Gen 4, and Cling. **World Models** like Google's Genie 2 and Nvidia's Cosmos simulate interactive environments for applications like robotics and autonomous driving.

**Coding Models/Agents** harness frontier AI for programming tasks. Cursor, Claude Code, OpenAI's Codex, Devon, and Factory are mentioned as powerful tools that can analyze codebases, write, and test code.

**Audio Models** cover voice and music generation. 11 Labs is highlighted for voice cloning and multilingual capabilities, while OpenAI's Voice Mode offers a conversational voice-first assistant. Various models exist for full song generation from a single prompt.
