## Key Takeaways

The core issue is a cash flow constraint from low-ticket monthly memberships with high ad acquisition costs.

*   **Focus on annual memberships upfront** during launch events to increase immediate cash collection and improve unit economics.

*   **Implement a two-stage "scoop-up" campaign**: sell annual with exclusive bonuses first, then retarget for monthly without those bonuses after the launch closes.

*   **Add high-value physical product bundles** (e.g., printer + paper) to increase perceived value, conversion rates, and allow for higher price points (up to $1,000).

## Summary

The business owner runs a membership for crafters (mostly women 45+) teaching sticker creation, generating over $1M annually with a $27/month or $270/year model. The primary scaling constraint is **30-day cash flow**: it costs about $90 in Meta ads to acquire a member, but they only collect $60 in the first 30 days, creating a cash deficit and a long (up to 6-month) payback period despite a good lifetime value (LTV) of around $300.

### The Proposed Scaling Solution

The advisor provides a clear, two-part strategy to solve the cash flow problem and enable profitable scaling.

**1. Restructure the Launch Offer**
During the multi-day paid launch events, the **only offer should be the annual membership ($270)**, packaged with 1-2 significant, **exclusive bonuses** not available elsewhere. This targets the consumer "impulse purchase window" of $300-$600, dramatically increasing the initial cash collected per new member.

**2. Implement a Post-Launch "Scoop-Up" Campaign**
After the launch cart closes, run a retargeting campaign to everyone who saw the ads but didn't buy. This second offer is for the **monthly membership ($27) without the key bonuses**. This captures price-sensitive customers while protecting the perceived value of the annual offer.

### Advanced Tactic: Physical Product Bundles

A further recommendation is to **add a tangible physical product** (like a sticker printer and paper kit) as a premium bundle. This gives customers a stronger "excuse" to justify the purchase, increases perceived value, and could justify raising the price point to $1,000, further accelerating cash recovery and scaling.
