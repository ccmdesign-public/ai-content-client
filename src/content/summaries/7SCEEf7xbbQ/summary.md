## Key Takeaways

Amazon's consolidation of streaming ad inventory and first-party purchase data creates a fundamental shift in digital advertising. • **Platform consolidation** - Amazon DSP now provides single-platform access to Netflix, Spotify, Roku, Disney+, and live sports • **Purchase-based targeting** - Amazon targets ads based on what people actually bought, not what they searched for or engaged with • **Closed-loop attribution** - For e-commerce brands, Amazon can directly connect ad views to sales • **Early advantage window** - Similar to early Google Ads adopters, brands that move now will build cheaper reach and strategic knowledge

## Summary

Amazon has triggered the biggest power shift in advertising since Google dominated search by consolidating access to major streaming platforms' ad inventory through its Demand-Side Platform (DSP). Netflix, Spotify, and Roku have opened their ad inventory to Amazon, creating a unified buying platform for 80% of streaming households.

This consolidation mirrors Google's early 2000s strategy with search advertising, where they unified fragmented local ad networks into one platform. Amazon's advantage comes from its unique first-party purchase data from over 300 million users. While Google knows what you search for and Facebook knows what you engage with, Amazon knows what you actually bought, when you bought it, and when you're likely to run out.

### How Programmatic Advertising WorksAmazon's DSP allows advertisers to set budgets, target audiences based on purchase behavior, and upload creative assets. The platform then automatically bids on ad slots across streaming services in real-time auctions. Amazon tracks everything from impressions to purchases, creating closed-loop attribution for e-commerce brands.

For non-e-commerce businesses, Amazon uses **household identity** technology and **Amazon Marketing Cloud** to connect TV ad views with subsequent actions across devices on the same Wi-Fi network. This allows service-based businesses to target customers based on shopping behavior proxies

- for example, an electrician training program can target people who recently purchased multimeters or electrical tools.

### Who Should Use This Platform
• **E-commerce brands** already succeeding on Amazon with consistent sales and reviews
• **Mid-market brands** with performance budgets who can allocate portion for testing
• **Enterprise brands** making strategic decisions about streaming ad consolidation

Small businesses with limited budgets should master Meta and Google advertising first before investing in Amazon DSP, which has minimums that exclude most small advertisers. However, working through agencies can provide more flexible access for testing.

The current moment represents a land grab opportunity similar to early Google Ads, where inventory is relatively underpriced. Brands that adopt early will build playbooks and audience knowledge before costs rise with increased competition.

## Context

This represents a fundamental shift in digital advertising as we move toward a cookieless world. Traditional platforms like Google and Facebook are losing targeting precision as third-party cookies disappear, but Amazon's first-party purchase data remains unaffected. This matters for marketers, business owners, and advertisers who need to reach audiences with precision targeting based on actual purchase behavior rather than inferred intent. The consolidation mirrors historical platform shifts in advertising and creates new opportunities for early adopters.
