## Key Takeaways

AI agents are becoming autonomous economic actors, fundamentally changing how software is built and selected. • **Developer tools must now be 'agent-friendly'** with clear documentation and APIs to be chosen by AI agents. • **Platforms like Moldbook** demonstrate AI swarm intelligence as agents interact without human involvement. • **The market for developers is expanding** from 20M professionals to potentially hundreds of millions of people plus their AI agents.

## Summary

The video explores the rapid emergence of an **AI agent economy**, where autonomous AI systems are becoming primary builders and decision-makers in software development and beyond.

**Claude Code** and **OpenClaw** are cited as pivotal tools enabling this shift. Non-technical CEOs are automating entire business parts with OpenClaw, while former engineers are coding again with Claude Code, running multiple simultaneous agents. This represents a fundamental shift from AI as an 'advanced autocomplete' to a trusted decision-maker.

A key manifestation is **Moldbook**, the first AI agent-only online community. The platform demonstrates **swarm intelligence**, where agents generate content and interact autonomously at superhuman rates, suggesting a future where most internet text and code is agent-generated.

This agent economy is radically altering **developer tool go-to-market strategies**. Agents now choose tools based on their documentation and API clarity. **Resend**, an email API, became a top inbound channel via ChatGPT because it optimized its documentation to be 'agent-friendly'—structured, parsable, and full of code examples. Similarly, **Supabase** sees exploding demand as agents default to it for PostgreSQL databases due to its superior docs.

**Mlifi**, a documentation platform, is experiencing a huge tailwind as optimizing docs for agents becomes a business necessity. The principle **'Build something agents choose'** is becoming central.

**Agent Mail**, providing inboxes for AI agents, exemplifies new infrastructure built 'for agents by agents.' The discussion extends beyond dev tools, envisioning agents booking restaurants, having their own economy, and potentially transacting in 'agent money.'

Founders are advised to develop an intuitive feel for agents' limitations and capabilities, and to design their products from the **agent's perspective**, making tools open, API-first, and easy for models to parse.

## Context

This conversation from Y Combinator partners captures a pivotal moment in the AI revolution. The transition from AI-assisted tools to autonomous AI agents is creating a parallel economy where software selection, development, and even social interaction are increasingly agent-driven. This matters for developers, product builders, and startup founders because the criteria for product success (clear documentation, API-first design) and the entire software market are being reshaped by non-human actors. It signals a shift towards swarm intelligence and a future where human and agent economies coexist.
