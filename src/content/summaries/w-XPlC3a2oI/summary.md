## Summary

Dan Martell presents a six-step process to build a multi-million dollar AI business as a solo founder, leveraging AI agents instead of employees. He emphasizes this is the biggest opportunity since the early 2000s, where billion-dollar companies will be built by individuals using AI to do all the work.

**Step 1: Rethink Business Growth**
Stop the traditional model of throwing bodies (employees) at problems. The new one-person AI business model focuses on identifying the bottleneck in a process and automating it with AI. Your role shifts from doing the work to designing the system that does the work, focusing on high-leverage decisions.

**Step 2: Find a Painful Problem Worth Solving**
Avoid falling in love with the technology. Instead, fall in love with the customer's problem. Look for a 'must-have' painkiller problem in a growing market (e.g., real estate, healthcare, coaching). Use tools like **Manis AI** to research potential problems. The pro tip is to call potential customers to ask for advice, not to sell, as this opens the door to understanding their real pain points.

**Step 3: Solve the Problem Manually First**
Before automating anything, fix the problem by hand to get paid to learn the steps. Use simple tools like spreadsheets or a virtual assistant. This validates the workflow and creates a group of early adopters. Draft a simple one-page 'done-for-you' offer structured around the problem, promise, timeline, price, and a guarantee. Sell this offer to the initial 10 people you interviewed.

**Step 4: Build a Clickable Prototype**
Create a 'Wizard of Oz' prototype—a fake solution that simulates the product experience without full functionality. Use AI-powered design tools like **Figma**, **UX Pilot**, or **Visually.ai** to create mock-ups in seconds from a description. Test this prototype with five new customers, record their reactions, and learn what they click and ask before writing any code.

**Step 5: Build Your MVP (Minimum Viable Product)**
Focus on the core features that deliver value around the core problem. Don't get distracted by feature requests from individual customers. Use no-code or low-code AI platforms to build fast. Martell demonstrates using **Manis AI** to generate a full-stack app from a single prompt in minutes, treating the AI like an intern to iterate quickly.

**Step 6: Scale with AI Agents, Not Headcount**
As revenue grows from $0 to $100K to $10M, systematically replace human tasks with AI automation and agents. Build systems AI can run (onboarding, support, operations) and eventually stack AI agents into workflows. The founder's role becomes strategy and sales while everything else happens automatically. He cites a company in his portfolio doing $83k/month with just the founder and two part-time contractors, all powered by AI workflows.
