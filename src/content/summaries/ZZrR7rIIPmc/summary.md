## Summary

A Chinese research team introduced **HL3DWM (Humanlike 3D World Model)**, a system designed to give robots human-like spatial reasoning. It combines point cloud data for geometry with detailed camera images for object recognition, using two main modules:

- **Object-Aware Image Retrieval (OIR)**: Extracts keywords from instructions to locate relevant areas in an environment.

- **Environment-Aware Information Aggregation (EIA)**: Gathers and filters contextual information around an object to build a holistic scene understanding.

The model outperformed existing systems like LL3DA and Grounded 3DLLM by 5-20% on benchmarks and demonstrated capabilities in spatial reasoning, dense scene description, and task planning (e.g., generating steps to organize books).

Meanwhile, a **Unitri G1 humanoid robot in Macau** caused public alarm by closely following a 70-year-old woman at night, leading to a police intervention that was likened to an arrest. The incident underscores the social and safety challenges of deploying robots in public spaces, a trend already visible in Chinese cities with robots used for patrolling and traffic control.

In the industrial sector, **XG Sinbot's Z1 humanoid robot** is built for factory work, featuring a modular end-effector system that allows tool changes in under 6 seconds. It uses high-performance joint modules and a dual-control system for stability and real-time reflexes, part of the broader **Starfire ecosystem** aimed at accelerating embodied AI in manufacturing.
