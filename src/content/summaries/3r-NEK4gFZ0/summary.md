## Summary

The speaker reveals that for a major launch, his team created between 2,000-3,000 different ad variations before the campaign even began. This massive volume of creative was essential for testing and optimization.

He predicts that advertising is moving decisively toward **hyperpersonalization**, where AI will enable businesses to create thousands of personalized ad permutations at unprecedented speed. This shift represents the move from broad targeting to **personalization at scale**, which he identifies as the dominant trend for 2026 and beyond.

The critical insight is that businesses must stop thinking about advertising as a linear team-building exercise and instead create **self-perpetuating systems**. The ideal model he describes is a "self-licking ice cream cone" where:
• Customers create content that attracts new customers
• Those new customers create more content
• The cycle continues without constant manual intervention

He notes that his portfolio companies are already seeing "crazy returns" from this approach, confirming it's not just theoretical but producing measurable business results today. The future belongs to companies that can build these automated creative ecosystems rather than those relying solely on expanding marketing teams.
