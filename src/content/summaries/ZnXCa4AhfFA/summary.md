## Key Takeaways

Ahrefs' May 2025 update focuses on enhancing SEO data visibility and reporting capabilities with seven key improvements:

- **Global keyword metrics** now show search volumes and traffic across all markets where keywords rank

- **Anonymous queries report** uncovers 46% of hidden Google Search Console keyword data with traffic volume and country stats

- **Paid keywords 3.0** finds 2x+ more keywords by checking SERP history up to 1 month back

- **Fully editable AI content topics** allow customization of content suggestions and topical coverage

## Summary

Ahrefs has released seven significant updates to their SEO platform in May 2025, focusing on improved data visibility, reporting capabilities, and content optimization tools.

**Report Builder Enhancements**
Users can now add Google Search Console (GSC) performance charts directly to reports, displaying total clicks, impressions, average CTR, and position metrics without switching between tools. More GSC widgets are planned for future releases.

**Web Analytics Improvements**
Tracked events in web analytics now include outbound link clicks, helping users understand what external content captures visitor attention. Users can drill down to see specific clicked links and identify which pages contain those links using the top pages widget.

**Site Explorer Upgrades**
Paid keywords 3.0 features significantly improved data logic that addresses the volatility of ad data. Previously only showing data from the latest SERP, the system now checks up to one month of historical data when current SERPs don't show targets. This new approach found almost 20,000 keywords for monday.com in the US compared to 9,700 previously, representing at least a two-times increase in coverage for most cases.

The organic keywords report now includes **global keyword metrics**, allowing users to gauge brand search demand across various markets. The global mode shows worldwide search volumes and traffic for each keyword, plus a list of all countries where the keyword ranks.

**AI Content Helper**
Topics within the AI content helper are now fully editable. Users can add new topics, modify existing ideas, delete unnecessary ones, and tap into AI suggestions to enhance topical coverage for content creation.

**Google Search Console Reporting**
A major breakthrough comes with the new **anonymous queries report**, which addresses Google Search Console's limitation of hiding nearly half of keyword data. Since 46% of all clicks come from queries marked as anonymous, this report helps uncover missing keywords along with traffic volume and country-level statistics by matching GSC data with Ahrefs' organic keywords report. GSC reports now have their own dedicated section with pinnable reports for quick navigation.

**Enterprise Features**
For enterprise users, tool access is now fully customizable. While admins and members get default access, guests require specific permission grants. Restricted tools appear grayed out in menus, and users without access see clear access denied messages.

## Context

This monthly product update from Ahrefs addresses critical gaps in SEO visibility and reporting that affect digital marketers, SEO professionals, and content creators. The updates are particularly significant because they tackle Google Search Console's limitation of hiding nearly half of all keyword data through anonymous queries, which has long frustrated SEO practitioners trying to understand their complete search performance. These improvements come at a time when businesses increasingly need global market insights and more comprehensive paid keyword data to compete effectively in search results.
