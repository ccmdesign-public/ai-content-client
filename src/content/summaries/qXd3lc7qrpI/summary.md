## Key Takeaways

Ubisoft's transformation reveals strategic shifts and potential impacts on The Division franchise.

• **Creative House Structure:** The Division is now part of Creative House 2 (competitive/cooperative shooters) alongside Ghost Recon and Splinter Cell, giving it more autonomy but also financial accountability.
• **Major Cost Cutting:** Ubisoft plans to reduce fixed costs by €200M over the next two years through studio closures, layoffs, and project cancellations, with six games already discontinued.
• **Project Delays & Cancellations:** Seven games have received additional development time (including an Assassin's Creed remake), while six were canceled including Prince of Persia: Sands of Time remake and four unannounced titles.
• **Organizational Timeline:** The new operating model begins in early April 2026, with more details expected at Ubisoft's February 12 investor meeting.

## Summary

Ubisoft has unveiled a comprehensive organizational reset designed to reclaim creative leadership and restore sustainable growth after years of financial struggles. The centerpiece is a new operating model built around five specialized **creative houses** that will have full creative, brand, and financial responsibility for their portfolios.

### The Division's New Home

The Division franchise has been placed in **Creative House 2**, which focuses on competitive and cooperative shooter experiences. This house also includes Ghost Recon and Splinter Cell IPs, all sharing Tom Clancy thematic elements. The creative house structure gives each unit end-to-end responsibility from development to publishing, including marketing and sales strategy.

### Financial Accountability & Autonomy

Each creative house will be financially accountable for its own P&L and cash generation, meaning The Division's success or failure won't be directly impacted by other Ubisoft properties performing poorly. This creates potential for more autonomy in decision-making but also removes corporate safety nets for underperforming projects.

### Cost Reduction & Studio Impact

Ubisoft confirmed aggressive cost-cutting measures, including:

- €200M in additional fixed cost reductions over the next two years

- Studio closures already implemented (Halifax mobile studio, Stockholm Studio)
- Further restructurings planned at Abu Dhabi, Red Links, and Massive Entertainment

- Strict hiring discipline across all functions

- Potential asset divestitures being considered

### Project Portfolio Changes

The company has significantly revised its three-year roadmap:

- **Six games discontinued** including Prince of Persia: Sands of Time remake and four unannounced titles (three new IPs, one mobile)
- **Seven games delayed** with additional development time, including an unannounced title pushed from fiscal year 2026 to 2027 (believed to be Assassin's Creed Black Flag remake)
- The Division 3 remains unaffected by current cancellations but could see release date adjustments

### Timeline & Next Steps

The new operating model begins implementation in early April 2026, with leadership structures for each creative house yet to be announced. More details are expected at Ubisoft's February 12 investor meeting. The creator expresses cautious optimism about the potential benefits for The Division's autonomy while acknowledging concern about the broader cost-cutting measures across Ubisoft.

## Context

This announcement comes after years of Ubisoft's financial struggles, including a €1 billion cash injection from Tencent in 2025 and widespread rumors of potential acquisition or shutdown. The video game industry has become increasingly competitive with rising development costs, making AAA publishers like Ubisoft reevaluate their organizational structures and project portfolios. This transformation matters to gamers, developers, and industry analysts as it signals a major shift in how one of the world's largest game publishers operates, with potential implications for beloved franchises like The Division, Assassin's Creed, and Far Cry.
