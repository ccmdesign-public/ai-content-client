## Summary

Theo from t3.gg issues a stark warning and call to action against new state-level legislation in California and Colorado that mandates age verification for operating system use. He contrasts the simplicity of early computing with the current regulatory landscape, which he argues is being shaped by lawmakers who fundamentally misunderstand technology.

### The Core Problem: Age Verification Laws

The primary focus is on California's AB-3080 and similar proposals. These laws will require operating system providers (like Windows, macOS, iOS, Android, and Linux distributions) to collect a user's age or birth date during initial device setup starting in 2027. This data must then be provided as an "age bracket" signal to applications. The laws carry severe financial penalties: up to $2,500 per affected minor and $7,500 for intentional violations.

Theo argues this creates an impossible situation for open-source software. Projects like Midnight BSD have already responded by updating their licenses to block users from California. He predicts many more will follow, either by geo-blocking or abandoning development due to the legal and logistical burden.

### Broader Implications and Dangers

This legislation is presented as part of a worrying trend under the banner of "online safety" that often leads to overreach. Theo draws parallels to EU attempts to weaken encryption. The specific dangers highlighted include:

*   **Privacy Erosion:** Apps will gain a new, persistent identifier (age bracket) without user consent, undoing some of the privacy protections platforms like iOS have implemented.

*   **Software Fragmentation:** If states enact differing rules (e.g., some requiring simple age entry, others mandating ID scans), developers will face a nightmare of compliance, potentially needing different software versions for different jurisdictions.

*   **Stifling Innovation:** The complexity and risk discourage developers, especially in open source, from building new operating systems or apps.

*   **Security Risks:** Forcing developers to handle sensitive ID data expands their attack surface and liability.

### A Call to Action for Developers

The video concludes by urging viewers, especially developers, to contact their state representatives. Theo provides resources like contactrep.org and emphasizes that lawmakers often lack basic understanding of software development. He stresses that informed constituent feedback from technical experts can be decisive in stopping poorly conceived laws before they spread to other states.
