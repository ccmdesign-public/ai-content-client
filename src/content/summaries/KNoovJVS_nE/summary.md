## Key Takeaways

The video introduces a four-layer diagnostic framework called the **failure stack** to systematically identify and fix AI failures. • **Layer 1: Input** – Check file size, complexity, and type; use spot-checks and grounding prompts to reduce hallucinations. • **Layer 2: Context** – Apply the **What, Why, How** framework for prompts and break complex tasks into smaller chunks. • **Layer 3: Model** – Different models have specific strengths; test multiple models to find the right tool for the job. • **Layer 4: The Wall** – For unsolvable tasks, break them down further, categorize what AI can/can't do, and maintain an **AI wish list** to revisit as models improve.

## Summary

The video presents a systematic diagnostic method called the **failure stack** to troubleshoot poor AI outputs from models like ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini. The framework consists of four layers that should be checked sequentially, from bottom to top, to identify the root cause of failure.

### Layer 1: Input

The first and most overlooked layer is the **input file**. Problems arise when files are too large, complex, or of a problematic type (e.g., PDFs with images and annotations). The AI may only process parts of the document, leading to incomplete or incorrect answers.

**Solutions:**
• **Spot-check:** Ask the AI to list headings and describe images from a file, then verify the output against your knowledge.
• **Reduce scope:** If only a subset of a large document matters, extract just those pages or instruct the AI to focus only on that section or specific topic.
• **Use grounding prompts:** Append these two sentences to your prompts: 1) "Base your answers only on what's in the file I gave you," and 2) "If you're unsure or the file doesn't contain enough information, say so instead of guessing."

### Layer 2: Context

This layer encompasses the **prompt**, associated context files, and the **complexity of the task**. A common failure is treating AI like a new employee given a vague sticky note instead of clear, detailed instructions.

**Solutions:**
• **Use the WWW Framework:** Structure prompts with **What** (the specific task), **Why** (your intent and why it matters), and **How** (constraints like format, tone, length).
• **Break down complex tasks:** If a task has 10+ steps and fails, break it into smaller chunks (e.g., 3 steps at a time) to increase the AI's chance of success.

### Layer 3: Model

Failure here means choosing the **wrong model for the task**. Each model has unique strengths. For example, Gemini might excel at parsing complex PDFs with visuals, while Claude might be better at matching a specific writing tone.

**Solution:** If you get poor results with one model, test the same input and prompt with another. The right tool can make all the difference.

### Layer 4: The Wall

The final layer is hitting the **current limits of AI capability**. If you've provided excellent input and context and tested multiple models with consistently bad results, the task may exceed today's AI.

**Solutions:**
• **Decompose the task further:** Break it into its smallest possible subtasks.
• **Categorize and create a wish list:** Identify which subtasks AI can and cannot handle. Add the impossible ones to an **AI wish list**.
• **Revisit the list monthly:** As new models and features are released, retest your wish list items. What was impossible six months ago may be achievable today.

The presenter emphasizes that **90% of AI failures can be fixed at the Input and Context layers**. By systematically applying this failure stack, users can diagnose issues in about 60 seconds and implement targeted solutions instead of giving up on AI.
