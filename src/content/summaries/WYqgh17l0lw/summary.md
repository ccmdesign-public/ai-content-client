## Key Takeaways

President Alexander Stubb presents a framework for understanding the current global geopolitical shift and how to navigate it.

*   We are in a **1918/1945/1989 moment** where the post-Cold War world order is changing, initiated by Russia's 2022 invasion of Ukraine and accelerated by the new US administration.

*   The world is divided into a **triangle of power**: the Global West (defending liberal order), the Global East (China-led, supporting multipolarity), and the decisive **Global South**.

*   The choice is between **multipolarity** (transactional, spheres of interest) and **multilateralism** (rules-based, institutional). Preserving the latter requires giving more agency to the Global South.

*   The practical foreign policy approach is **'values-based realism'**: staying true to democratic values while pragmatically engaging with all actors to solve global problems, coupled with a **'dignified foreign policy'** of respect.

## Summary

Finnish President Alexander Stubb frames the current era as a fundamental shift in the world order, comparable to the moments after World War I, World War II, and the Cold War. The catalyst for this transition was Russia's full-scale invasion of Ukraine in February 2022, which has been further accelerated by changes in US foreign policy.

The global landscape is now defined by a **triangle of power**. The **Global West**, traditionally led by the US, seeks to preserve the existing liberal international order. The **Global East**, led by China, advocates for a multipolar world. The **Global South**, comprising roughly 125 countries, is positioned as the decisive bloc that will shape what comes next.

Stubb posits that the core ideological struggle is between **multipolarity** and **multilateralism**. Multipolarity is transactional and based on spheres of influence, reminiscent of 19th-century power politics. Multilateralism is rooted in international institutions, rule of law, and norms. To defend the multilateral, liberal order, Western nations must reform it by giving more power and agency to countries in the Global South who feel the current system was built primarily in the West's image eighty years ago.

### Navigating the Transition with Values-Based Realism

Stubb's proposed method for operating in this uncertain world is **'values-based realism'**. This means adhering to core values like democracy, human rights, and the rule of law, while being pragmatic and realistic that not all global challenges can be solved exclusively with like-minded allies. It necessitates engaging with a wider range of actors. This is complemented by a **'dignified foreign policy'**, which emphasizes treating all nations with respect, a principle he feels is currently lacking.

He applies this framework to current crises. On the **Greenland crisis** (a recent major transatlantic rift), the strategy involved a mix of showing seriousness with potential countermeasures while engaging in 'frenetic diplomacy' behind the scenes to de-escalate and find an off-ramp.

Regarding **Ukraine**, Stubb is 'carefully optimistic' about peace talks, noting progress in distilling plans. He vehemently counters the narrative that Russia is winning, arguing Putin has failed all his strategic aims: Ukraine is moving toward the EU, NATO has enlarged with Finland and Sweden, and Europe is remilitarizing. He asserts Russia continues the war only because 'this war is too big for Putin to fail,' given its economic toll.

### The Future of European Integration

On **EU enlargement**, Stubb argues it has become a strategic, existential necessity rather than a purely legal process. He suggests the Union may need to adopt a more **flexible, 'variable geometry' approach**, potentially granting Ukraine membership first and then working through the legislative chapters afterward. This recognizes Ukraine's immense value as a future member, which would dramatically increase the EU's military power, despite the absorption challenges.

## Context

This analysis is crucial for understanding the high-stakes reordering of global power dynamics in the 2020s. It matters to policymakers, business leaders, and engaged citizens navigating an era marked by war, shifting alliances, and ideological competition between rules-based systems and transactional power blocs. Stubb provides a clear conceptual framework from a key European leader directly involved in managing these crises.
