## Summary

Anthropic has launched **Claude Co-work Dispatch**, a feature that allows users to initiate a co-work session on their computer and then monitor progress and provide approvals remotely via their mobile device. The session remains hosted in a sandbox on the user's computer for security. Early users describe it as a 'walkie-talkie' or remote control for Claude, noting it covers much of the functionality people sought from OpenClaw but with greater perceived stability and safety due to existing integrations. The broader trend is the convergence of features like Code, Co-work, and Dispatch into a **persistent AI layer** that follows users across devices.

Separately, **OpenClaw is facing significant regulatory scrutiny in China**. Government agencies have warned staff not to install it, issued guidelines (like using official versions and minimizing permissions), and there are reports of officials attempting to bar executives from Chinese AI firm Manis from leaving the country amid Meta's acquisition. Public sentiment is shifting from excitement to **'AI anxiety'**, driven largely by fears of job displacement as media reports highlight OpenClaw's autonomous capabilities.

On the infrastructure front, **Nvidia is restarting production of H200 chips for the Chinese market** after export approvals, with reports of Chinese firms ordering 'hundreds of thousands' of chips. CEO Jensen Huang forecasts the company could see a trillion dollars in sales by 2027. Furthermore, **Amazon CEO Andy Jassy told staff that AI could double AWS revenue** to $600 billion over the long term, signaling a belief that the cloud market is entering a massive second growth phase driven by AI.
