## Summary

Ryan Stewart returns to YouTube after a year-long hiatus, explaining that recent AI developments—specifically Claude's capabilities—represent such a fundamental shift in agency work that he felt compelled to resume creating content.

### The Game-Changing Example

He provides a concrete example: his agency previously spent two months and $10,000 in contractor fees building a Facebook ads dashboard that pulled API data from Facebook Ads and client CRMs into Looker. This system was constantly breaking and required ongoing maintenance.

Using **Claude Co-work**, they were able to rebuild, improve, and automate the same dashboard in just 15 minutes. The new version pulls Facebook ads data, CRM data, and even organic Facebook/Instagram data into a single automated dashboard that runs on a schedule.

### Agency Business Transformation

Stewart believes this represents the biggest change in the agency space since the shift from traditional to digital marketing. The ability to automate workflows at this unprecedented pace will fundamentally change agency economics and structure.

He predicts that within 6-12 months: • One-person businesses will generate millions in revenue • Agencies will shrink to 1-3 person teams focused on strategy and client relationships • Traditional agency models that rely on large execution teams will become obsolete

### The New Agency Role

The human element remains crucial, but shifts from execution to: • **Subject matter expertise** and strategic thinking • **Client communication** and relationship management • **Technology oversight** - knowing what to build and how to leverage AI tools

Clients will still pay agencies to leverage this technology because most businesses won't want to figure out or manage these complex AI systems themselves.

### Content Approach

Stewart announces he'll be creating daily, unedited videos showing real workflows from his agency business using Claude tools. He criticizes much existing AI content as being created by people who aren't actually using the technology for real business cases, promising to show practical applications rather than theoretical discussions.
