## Summary

**Google's LIIA 3 Music Generator**

Google has launched LIIA 3, the latest version of DeepMind's music generation model, directly accessible within the Gemini app. Unlike competitors like Suno, LIIA 3 accepts multimodal inputs—text, images, or video—to generate 30-second music clips. Lyrics can be generated in eight languages. The feature is also being integrated into YouTube's Dream Track for creating soundtracks for Shorts, with custom cover art generated by Nano Banana. While limited to short clips and not suitable for full songs, Google positions it as a fun, social feature for personal expression, embedding Synth ID watermarks for AI detection.

**Anthropic's OOTH Token Controversy**

Anthropic updated its terms to prohibit using OOTH tokens (similar to API keys for subscriptions) from free or Pro/Max accounts in third-party products, including the Agent SDK. This sparked significant backlash from users powering tools like OpenClaw, leading to confusion about personal use versus commercial applications. Anthropic's Shapard clarified the intent was to force businesses to pay via the API, not block personal tinkering, but the incident highlighted concerns about walled gardens and the future of modular AI use cases.

**Meta's Revived Smartwatch Project**

Meta has revived its smartwatch project under the codename Malibu 2, after initially canceling it in 2022. The watch is reported to include health tracking, a built-in Meta AI assistant, and two cameras—one in the dial for video calls and one underneath for photos. It may also read nerve signals for use as a controller. This move puts Meta in direct competition with Apple and Google in the wearable AI category, as part of a broader strategy focusing on a concentrated set of AR/VR products.

**xAI's Grok Heavy with 16 Sub-Agents**

xAI announced a new version of Grok Heavy that uses 16 sub-agents to debate responses before delivering a final answer, an expansion from the 4 sub-agents in Grok 4.2. An example query on "How does chaos birth cosmic order?" resulted in a 700-word report with nearly 900 references after over a minute of debate. While the practical utility of this approach is mixed, it represents an ongoing experiment in multi-agent reasoning within AI models.

**Chinese AI Models: Benchmarks vs. Reality**

Flo Crell, founder of Lindy, shared insights on Chinese AI models, noting a significant gap between their performance on benchmarks and real-world, agentic tasks. He suggests these models may be distilled from frontier models, trained specifically for evaluations, or potentially use stolen weights, resulting in "shallow intelligence." Crell advises taking high benchmark scores with a grain of salt and emphasizes the importance of hands-on testing, a lesson applicable to Western models as well.
