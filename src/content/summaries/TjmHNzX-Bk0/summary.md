## Key Takeaways

The video analyzes a viral AI thought experiment and its real-world implications for tech business models. Key takeaways include: • **AI agents threaten SaaS** by making in-house development viable, forcing vendors to compete on deeper value. • **Consumer habitual loyalty is obsolete** as AI agents will comparison-shop across all available options. • **Middleman business models are vulnerable** where AI can automate away friction and optimize for price.

## Summary

This video dissects a viral fictional memo from Catrini Research titled "The 2028 Global Intelligence Crisis," which sparked market anxiety by depicting a near-future where AI disrupts foundational tech business models.

The first theme is the **threat to SaaS companies**. The memo describes a world where **agentic coding tools** like those from OpenAI and Anthropic enable Fortune 500 companies to build custom internal solutions, bypassing vendors like Monday.com, Asana, and Zapier. This shift from 'buy' to 'build' is already visible, with Asana's stock down 62% and companies using Claude plugins for tasks like project management and data analysis.

The second theme is the **end of habitual loyalty** in consumer apps. Using DoorDash as a poster child, the memo argues that AI agents won't default to a single app on a home screen. Instead, they will simultaneously check all available services (DoorDash, Uber Eats, direct restaurant sites) to find the cheapest and fastest option, commoditizing the service. DoorDash's CTO acknowledged this shift, emphasizing the need to build a 'compelling ecosystem for agents.'

The third and broadest theme is the **death of the middleman**. The memo suggests any business model built on taking a cut between two parties—due to human limitations like laziness or impatience—is vulnerable. It highlights the payments industry (Mastercard, Visa) where AI agents could route transactions via stablecoins to avoid interchange fees. Stripe's annual letter on the 'five levels of agentic commerce' aligns with this vision, forecasting a future where AI agents handle purchasing autonomously.

The video also covers recent AI product developments: **Perplexity Computer** (a cloud digital worker that chains multi-step actions), **Claude Code's 'remote work' feature**, and **Cursor's 'demo' feature** for delegating user journey development to AI agents. Finally, it mentions Anthropic's 'Code Modernization Playbook' report and a design analysis ('The Aesthetics of AI') dissecting the visual trends of 23 AI companies.

## Context

This discussion matters because it highlights the profound vulnerability of modern tech business models to AI-driven automation and agentic behavior. Product managers, founders, and investors need to understand that AI isn't just a feature—it's a fundamental force that can erase moats built on convenience, friction, and information asymmetry. The viral memo, while fictional, reflects real market fears and aligns with trends like the rise of AI coding assistants and the growth of stablecoin payments, forcing a reevaluation of what creates durable value in a software-driven economy.
