## Summary

The video presents a March Madness-style bracket tournament evaluating 16 different AI agents and projects built by the host in early 2026. The competition highlights the rapid shift towards **agentic AI systems** and the practical experimentation happening within the community.

The tournament was judged on a subjective mix of technical complexity, daily usefulness, broader value, and personal fondness. Agents were seeded into brackets by both Claude and ChatGPT, which produced nearly identical rankings.

### Notable Agent Finalists

**Mycroft** emerged as the overall champion. Modeled after Sherlock Holmes's smarter older brother, Mycroft is a **digital chief AI officer** that lives primarily in Slack. It conducts discovery interviews with users to build and continuously update a comprehensive AI strategy and roadmap for an entire company, covering use cases, data integration, governance, upskilling, and ROI goals. Its recommendations are kept current by pulling external information from the 221B knowledge hub.

**Chucky**, inspired by the movie *Good Will Hunting*, was a close runner-up. It acts as an **agent builder's representative**, allowing creators to send an interactive agent instead of a static portfolio to potential clients or employers. Chucky can pull information about different applications the builder has created, share screenshots and context, and provide a visualization of their full agent ecosystem.

**Holmes**, another Sherlock-themed agent, is a **personal AI advisor** with a web interface and Slack presence. It interviews individuals about their work and AI usage, builds a detailed 'case file' on them, and provides personalized AI tool recommendations. These recommendations are also automatically updated weekly based on data from 221B.

### Supporting Systems and Other Projects

The **221B knowledge hub** is the critical backend engine that powers agents like Holmes and Mycroft. It automatically ingests podcast transcripts, performs web searches on key platforms, conducts weekly interviews with the host, and compiles dossiers on enterprise AI trends.

Other highlighted projects included the **Witty Radars Researcher**, a persistent OpenClaw agent that hunts for new AI use case studies 24/7; the **Agent Madness bracket platform** itself; **Claw Camp** and **AIDB New Year's**, which are self-directed learning programs; and **Super Intelligent Compass**, a power tool for AI adoption professionals.

The host concludes that while many projects are experiments, the most valuable agents are those that provide persistent, customized, and actionable value, moving beyond one-time assessments to become ongoing strategic partners.
