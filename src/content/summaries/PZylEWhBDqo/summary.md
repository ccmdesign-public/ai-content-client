## Summary

The creator achieved explosive growth for his app, Yorby, a social media marketing platform, by using the app's own AI to fuel its marketing. The core strategy was organic social media marketing using a specific video format.

### The 'Hook and Demo' Video FormatThe entire marketing effort centered on a simple, repeatable video format. Each video started with a **juicy hook**—a curiosity-sparking statement like 'POV: you finally understand why creators stopped using ChatGPT'—generated using Yorby's AI, which was trained on a database of viral marketing content. This hook presented a problem. The following clip was a **demo of Yorby** showing the solution. This format proved highly effective on platforms like TikTok and Instagram.

### Eating Your Own Dog Food

A key factor was using their own product to market itself. The technical founder and his business co-founder used Yorby's AI content studio to brainstorm hooks and remix viral video formats for their own niche. This validated the product's utility and created a powerful feedback loop.

### Scaling the Strategy: AI vs. Human ContentAfter initial success with AI-generated content, they began hiring human UGC creators to replicate the winning format. The rationale was leveraging **time versus money**; they used new revenue to pay marketers, treating content creation as a 'venture capital play' where one viral account out of many can drive the majority of growth. They concluded that the algorithm cares about content quality, not its origin (AI or human).

### Capturing Data and Future ChannelsDespite the rapid growth, they prioritized capturing user email addresses on signup for future marketing, using Brevo for its developer-friendly API and interface. While short-form video drove ~80% of growth, LinkedIn was used to attract a different, potentially more enterprise-oriented audience. Future plans include doubling down on organic video, exploring paid Meta ads via an agency, and eventually implementing a technical SEO strategy.

## Context

This case study is highly relevant for indie hackers, startup founders, and product marketers looking for low-budget, high-impact growth strategies. It demonstrates the power of 'dogfooding' your own product, the effectiveness of a simple, repeatable content format, and how to leverage AI tools not just as a product feature but as a core component of the marketing engine itself. It connects to broader trends of AI-powered content creation and organic social media as a primary growth channel for SaaS products.
