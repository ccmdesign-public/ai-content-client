## Key Takeaways

This video presents a data-driven framework for setting prices based on sales close rates for businesses with sales teams. The core idea is that exceptionally high close rates indicate significant underpricing, creating a systematic ladder for price adjustments.

*   A **close rate above 80%** typically means you're underpriced by **3 to 4 times** your current price.

*   A close rate **below 30%** signals problems with your target customer (**avatar**) or sales process, which should be fixed before lowering price.

*   For most non-software service businesses, the only sustainable direction is to **continuously raise prices** to improve margins, reputation, and talent.

## Summary

The video provides a practical, rule-of-thumb framework for business owners to determine if they are charging the right price based on their sales close rate. The presenter, drawing from years of business experience, establishes a direct correlation between how many prospects buy and how much you can charge.

### The Pricing-Close Rate Ladder

The framework is structured as a tiered ladder where each close rate range corresponds to a specific level of underpricing:

*   **Close Rate >80%:** You are likely underpriced by **3 to 4 times**. If you close four out of five leads, your price is too low.

*   **Close Rate 60-80%:** You are likely underpriced by **2 to 3 times**.

*   **Close Rate 50-60%:** You are likely underpriced by **1.5 to 2 times**.

*   **Close Rate 40-50%:** You are likely underpriced by **1.25 to 1.5 times**.

*   **Close Rate 30-40%:** This is considered **appropriately priced**, assuming you have a proper sales motion where leads are educated before the sales call.

*   **Close Rate <30%:** This indicates a fundamental problem—either you are selling to the wrong customer (**avatar issue**) or your sales process is broken. The solution is to fix these issues, not to lower your price.

### The Exception for Scalable vs. Unscalable Businesses

The presenter makes a critical distinction between business models. For **SaaS or software companies** (about 5% of businesses), pricing is a balance between growth rate and gross margin. Lowering prices can boost growth, while raising them improves margins.

However, for the vast majority (**80%**) of businesses, which are **service-based and unscalable**, the only long-term direction is **up**. As you improve your skills and reputation, demand increases, but your capacity to serve is limited. Therefore, you must raise prices. This creates a virtuous cycle: higher prices lead to better margins, which allows you to hire better talent and attract better clients, further justifying higher prices. Conversely, lowering prices leads to a vicious cycle of worse margins, worse talent, and worse clients.

It moves beyond gut feeling to a quantitative metric—the close rate—which directly reflects market demand and perceived value. This is crucial for any business owner, founder, or sales leader looking to maximize profitability without leaving money on the table or driving away customers.
