## Summary

The video argues that most CRM reviews focus on the wrong metrics—features, pricing, and integrations—which is why businesses switch every 18 months. Instead, the presenter introduces a three-criteria **CRM decision framework** to determine if a system will save or strangle your business.

### The Three Critical Criteria

**1. Integration Load:** This is the number of separate systems you need to duct-tape together. Every integration is a point of failure—like a broken Zapier zap that can cost you a deal. The presenter lost a $12,000 deal this way. The cognitive load of context-switching between tools can cost up to 40% of productive time. The ideal integration load is zero, with everything in one place.

**2. Response Speed:** It’s not just about being fast; it’s about what happens in a prospect's mind between interest and contact. A Harvard study found responding within 5 minutes makes you **100 times more likely** to connect than waiting 30+ minutes. The key question for a CRM is: can it respond automatically within 60 seconds without manual intervention?

**3. True Cost:** This isn't just the subscription fee. It's the total cost of the stack (CRM, email, SMS, scheduler, forms, payments), plus the tools needed to make it work (like Zapier), plus the time spent managing it, plus revenue lost when it fails. A typical 'Frankenstack' can cost over $212/month before accounting for hours of management and training new staff on seven different logins.

### The Recommended Solution: High LevelAfter years of testing, the presenter found **High Level** meets all three criteria. It has an integration load of zero, bundling CRM, email, SMS, phone, calendar, funnels, forms, payments, and more into one platform. Response speed is built-in, with automations that send texts within 60 seconds of a form submission. The true cost is $97/month for a small agency, compared to the $212+ of a fragmented stack.
