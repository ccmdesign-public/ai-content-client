## Summary

The creator details their 2026 AI coding workflow, highlighting significant shifts in how they leverage AI agents compared to previous years. The workflow emphasizes a pragmatic approach, combining specialized AI models and tools to optimize different development stages.

### AI IDEs and Models

The primary AI IDE remains **Cursor**, used mainly for its superior tab-to-complete functionality. While Cursor offers advanced features like built-in browser and debugging, the creator primarily uses these sparingly, favoring a simpler approach. For agentic coding, **Claude Code with Opus 4.5** is the main tool, valued for its overall performance. However, for specific tasks, other models are integrated within Cursor.

**GPT-5.2 Codecs** is reserved for large, time-intensive refactors due to its ability to handle extensive context, despite being slower. For quicker, in-the-moment coding assistance, Cursor's in-house **Composer 1** model (believed to be based on GLM) is preferred for its speed. **Gemini 3 Pro** stands out for UI/UX tasks, consistently delivering superior design outputs even with vague instructions, making it ideal for generating design languages from screenshots.

### Workflow Strategies

A core strategy is to always use a "plan mode" before execution, prompting the AI to think and outline its approach. This practice, inspired by research suggesting improved output with deliberation, significantly enhances the quality of AI-generated solutions. The creator avoids "one-shotting" complex features, finding that breaking down tasks into smaller, manageable steps, and delegating specific code writing to LLMs while maintaining architectural oversight, yields better and less time-consuming results than fixing overly ambitious AI outputs.

### Parallel Agent Workflow

To boost productivity and manage the slower response times of powerful AI models, the creator employs a **parallel agent workflow** using **Git worktrees**, facilitated by the open-source tool **Branchlit**. This allows working on multiple isolated branches simultaneously, preventing merge conflicts and enabling concurrent development. For Superbase applications, separate directories and local Superbase instances with distinct ports are used to manage isolated database changes across different tasks.

### No-Code Integration

Finally, the workflow incorporates **no-code/low-code tools** to accelerate development velocity and enable contributions from non-technical team members. Mind Studio is specifically mentioned for powering significant portions of the creator's startup, Yori, particularly for data collection, categorization, and saving to Superbase. This approach prioritizes speed and efficiency by leveraging existing solutions rather than rebuilding everything from scratch.
