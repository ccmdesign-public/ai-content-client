## Key Takeaways

Google's Anti-Gravity platform received a significant update focused on model access and quota management. The key takeaways are: • **Gemini 3.1 Pro** is now available and performs well, but its **quota depletes rapidly** even for Pro subscribers • A **hidden quota tracker** was added in settings, allowing users to monitor usage for Gemini and external models like Anthropic's Opus and Sonnet • Previously, users needed third-party plugins to see quota status, now it's built-in but needs better visibility

## Summary

Google has released an update for its Anti-Gravity AI platform that introduces two major changes centered around model access and quota transparency.

**Gemini 3.1 Pro Availability and Quota Issues**
The update makes **Gemini 3.1 Pro** available through Anti-Gravity, and initial performance feedback is positive. However, users are experiencing a significant problem: the **quota for Gemini Pro depletes very quickly**, even for Pro subscribers. This limitation forces users to wait for quota resets (either 5-hour or weekly windows) once they hit their limits.

**Hidden Quota Tracking Feature**
The most important addition is a **built-in quota tracking system** that shows remaining usage for both Google's Gemini models and external AI models integrated into Anti-Gravity. This includes models from Anthropic (**Opus** and **Sonnet**) and potentially OpenAI models.

**Location and Usability**
The quota information is currently **hidden in settings** rather than being prominently displayed. Users must navigate to User Settings > Models to access this data. The presenter suggests Google should make this information more visible, similar to how third-party plugins previously displayed quota status at the bottom of the interface.

**Comparison to Previous Workflow**
Before this update, users had to rely on **third-party extensions and plugins** to monitor their quota usage. The new built-in system provides official quota information directly from Google, eliminating the need for external tools but requiring more navigation to access the data.

**Overall Assessment**
Anti-Gravity remains a valuable tool for accessing multiple AI models (Gemini and Opus) in one platform, with Cursor being mentioned as the only other tool offering similar multi-model access. The presenter hopes Google will increase quota allocations for Pro subscribers given the rapid consumption rates observed with Gemini Pro.

## Context

This update matters for developers and AI practitioners using Google's Anti-Gravity platform to access multiple AI models through a single interface. As AI model usage becomes more integrated into development workflows, quota management and transparency become critical for productivity. The rapid quota depletion with Gemini Pro highlights ongoing challenges with AI service pricing and accessibility, while the hidden quota tracker represents progress toward better usage monitoring in multi-model AI platforms.
