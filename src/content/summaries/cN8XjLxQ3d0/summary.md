• **Diagnose the constraint first** – The business is currently **demand-constrained**, not capacity-constrained, meaning the core problem is generating enough qualified leads.
• **Double down on what works** – The founder’s strength is **outbound sales** and **conference networking**; the advice is to systemize these through a dedicated **SDR** (Sales Development Representative) and a structured conference playbook.
• **Fix the marketing function** – Replace “keep the lights on” marketing with actionable, lead-generating activities like **thought leadership content** on LinkedIn that showcases client problem-solving.

## Summary

The video is a coaching session where Alex Hormozi advises the owner of a mid-market cybersecurity compliance consulting firm. The business has seen revenue drop from nearly $5 million in 2023 to $3.6 million, with net margins shrinking from 35% to 18%. The owner attributes this to shifting focus from sales (being a “hunter”) to internal CEO duties like people management and recruiting, which created a “people debt” that consumed margins.

### Identifying the Core Bottleneck

Hormozi immediately identifies the business as **demand-constrained**. The owner confirms they can handle more customers, so the primary issue is lead generation. The historical sales motion relied heavily on the founder’s personal outbound efforts and some conference networking, but conference costs have risen dramatically, making that channel less efficient.

### The Prescribed Scaling Strategy

Hormozi outlines a three-part plan to rebuild the sales pipeline:

1.  **Re-engage with conferences strategically.**
    *   The advice is to return to physical conferences but with a proven “conference playbook” that maximizes lead capture.

*   The goal is to “fish with a net” – collect as many leads as possible and create urgency with a structured offer.

*   A target of **two conferences per month** is suggested as a sustainable pace.

2.  **Hire a Sales Development Representative (SDR).**
    *   This is highlighted as the largest time-saver for the founder. An SDR would handle the initial outbound prospecting and qualification, allowing the founder to focus solely on closing deals.

*   Hormozi suggests the owner’s current recruiter, who wants to move into technical sales, could be a perfect fit for this SDR role, as recruiting outbound skills are highly transferable.

3.  **Leverage LinkedIn for thought leadership.**
    *   Instead of generic “keep the lights on” marketing, the owner should publicly share insights from their client work.

*   The method is simple: “take the stuff you're doing privately and then just talk about it publicly” to attract a relevant audience and build credibility.

The session concludes with a promotion for Hormozi’s **$100 Million Scaling Roadmap**, a free diagnostic tool that helps business owners identify their growth stage and key actions across all business functions.
