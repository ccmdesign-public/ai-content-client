## Key Takeaways

The AI Daily Brief's January survey of 583 advanced users shows fundamental shifts in AI adoption patterns and value perception.

*   **Time savings is no longer the primary benefit** – replaced by increased output (38%) and new capabilities (22%), signaling a move beyond efficiency to transformation

*   **Claude has captured the builder segment** – 45.8% of respondents chose it as their primary model, with Claude users being heavier, more agentic (52%), and reporting higher value gains

*   **Vibe coding has gone mainstream** – 69% use it, with 49.5% of coding work coming from outside engineering roles, fundamentally redrawing job boundaries

*   **Agentic adoption has crossed a threshold** – 37.6% reported agentic AI use, with leadership roles (57% of C-suite) driving adoption, suggesting organizational permission structures are forming

## Summary

### Survey Context and MethodologyThe AIDB Intel January AI Usage Pulse surveyed 583 highly engaged AI users from The AI Daily Brief audience, representing an advanced, forward-looking subset rather than the average user. 97.6% use AI daily, with 43% spending over 10 hours weekly. The sample included diverse roles and company sizes, with 38% from small companies (0-50 people) and 27% from large enterprises (5,000+ employees).

### Model Landscape and Claude DominanceUsers are **polyamorous with models**, averaging 3.5 different models with only 5% using a single model. While ChatGPT has broader reach (87% usage), **Claude is the preferred primary model** at 45.8%, compared to 31% for ChatGPT and 16% for Gemini.

Claude primary users exhibit distinct characteristics:

*   53% use AI 10+ hours weekly (vs. 40% of ChatGPT users)
*   52% report agentic AI usage (vs. 24% of ChatGPT users)
*   87% use vibe coding tools (vs. 52% of ChatGPT users)
*   88% report increased value (vs. 74% of ChatGPT users)

### Shift in Benefits and Value PerceptionThe most significant finding is the **transition away from time savings as the primary benefit**. In the previous AI ROI survey, 76.7% cited time savings as a primary benefit, but in January it dropped to third place (20%).

Current primary benefits:

*   Increased output/throughput: 38%
*   New capabilities: 22%
*   Time savings: 20%
*   Improved decision-making: 11%
*   Improved quality: 8%

Heavier users (10+ hours weekly) show even more pronounced shifts – only 10% cite time savings as primary benefit, while 49% cite increased output and 27% cite new capabilities.

### Agentic Adoption and Vibe Coding Revolution
**Agentic AI usage has reached 37.6%**, a dramatic increase from 14% in November's AI ROI survey. Agentic users tend to be heavier users (57% in 10+ hour category), use more models (3.8 average), and cite new capabilities as their top benefit.

**Vibe coding has become ubiquitous** with 69% adoption and another 21% interested in trying. Most significantly, **49.5% of coding work comes from outside engineering and IT roles**, including 34% of executives, 13% of product roles, and 11% of operations roles.

### Momentum and BarriersUsage and value are accelerating – 71% increased usage month-over-month, and 83% reported increased value. Even among those with flat usage, 63% still reported increasing value, suggesting skills development and learning curve benefits.

Key barriers remain:

*   Not enough time to learn: top issue

*   Skills gap: 18% don't know how to use AI effectively

*   Policy/approval barriers: 17%
*   Access to right tools: 10%

Organizational stance significantly impacts adoption – 47% at encouraging organizations spend 10+ hours weekly vs. only 29% at restrictive organizations.

### Future Implications and Organizational ImpactThe survey suggests **job roles are being fundamentally redrawn** as non-coders become builders through AI. Organizational charts, hiring criteria, and training requirements are all in flux.

For AI strategy, the data suggests prioritizing:

*   Infrastructure for agentic workflows

*   Tooling and governance frameworks

*   Data access policies

*   Training resources to bridge skills gaps

## Context

This survey provides critical insights into how advanced AI users are evolving beyond initial efficiency gains to transformational applications. As early adopters often signal broader market trends, these findings help organizations understand where AI value is shifting – from time savings to increased output, new capabilities, and agentic workflows. The data reveals fundamental changes in how work gets done, who can do it, and what skills matter in an AI-augmented workforce.
