## Key Takeaways

The video presents a systematic approach to prevent AI-driven project failures by validating ideas before development. • **AI plans are inherently over-ambitious** – they look impressive but often include unnecessary features that derail projects. • **The Strip Test** identifies core vs. nice-to-have features using AI as a 'ruthless product manager' to prioritize what's essential. • **The Lynchpin Test** validates that the AI can actually perform the critical task before any development begins. • **The Price Test** ensures economic viability by testing with cheaper models and calculating operational costs.

## Summary

The creator shares a painful lesson: when asking AI to create a plan for building software or automation, the resulting plan is often too ambitious and sets you up for failure. This happens because AI naturally adds numerous 'nice-to-have' features that distract from the core value, leading to wasted days or weeks on unbuildable projects.

To combat this, a simple 30-minute, three-test checklist was developed. **The Strip Test** involves taking the AI-generated plan and using another AI instance (with a specific prompt) to act as a 'ruthless product manager.' This AI categorizes every feature as either 'core' (the product is useless without it) or 'later' (adds value but isn't essential for the initial workflow). This forces focus on the minimal viable version.

**The Lynchpin Test** addresses the critical dependency of AI capabilities. For any automation centered on an AI task (e.g., extracting data from PDFs), you must manually test that specific task in a chat interface (like ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini) before writing any code. The process is: identify the lynchpin task, test it with a basic prompt, refine the prompt and context if it fails, and loop until it works or you conclude it's impossible with current models.

**The Price Test** tackles operational economics. If the lynchpin test succeeds with a top-tier (expensive) model, you must attempt to replicate the results with a cheaper, less intelligent model by further optimizing the prompt and context. Additionally, you can use AI to estimate monthly API costs based on your expected usage volume, ensuring the project is financially sustainable before development.

## Context

As businesses and individuals increasingly use AI to plan and build software automations, a critical failure point has emerged in the planning phase. Unlike traditional development where problems arise during building or testing, AI-generated plans often look deceptively perfect but are fundamentally flawed with scope creep and unrealistic assumptions. This video provides a crucial framework for product managers, entrepreneurs, and developers who use AI as a co-pilot, helping them validate ideas, ensure technical feasibility, and maintain economic viability before investing significant development time.
