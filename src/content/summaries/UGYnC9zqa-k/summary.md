## Key Takeaways

Sasabi is an AI-native observability platform that aims to automate debugging and maintenance for engineering teams. Its core principles include: **Logs are all you need** – leveraging AI to extract insights from unstructured logs, eliminating the need for separate metrics and traces. **Built for the AI era** – designed from 2026 onwards to use AI agents for real-time system analysis. **Focus on maintenance** – addressing the larger part of software engineering (maintaining existing systems) rather than just creating new code.

## Summary

Sherwood, a second-time YC founder, introduces Sasabi, an observability platform built for the "AI era" starting in 2026. The company's foundational philosophy is **"logs are all you need,"** a controversial departure from the traditional **three pillars of observability** (logs, metrics, traces).

**Why logs alone?** Historically, logs were less valuable due to their unstructured nature. However, AI agents can now read and interpret log lines, extracting meaningful insights. This simplifies instrumentation for engineering teams, as every developer inherently knows how to use print statements and read log streams.

**The Problem and Vision** The idea stemmed from Sherwood's personal frustration during outages at companies like Brex, where he spent hours manually digging through dashboards. While AI coding tools (like Cursor) have revolutionized creating new software, the maintenance side hasn't changed. Sasabi aims to automate this, allowing developers to ask natural language questions (e.g., "Why is production down?" or "Which commit is responsible?") and get immediate answers.

**Founder Journey and Lessons** Sherwood's first company, Opkit, was a healthcare fintech startup built on an "MBA case study" approach, not personal passion or expertise. This led to a challenging journey. For Sasabi, he deliberately aligned the company with his background, interests, and experience in observability, DevOps, and selling to tech startups. He emphasizes building a **"tombstone company"**—something you are uniquely suited to build.

**Future Vision** Sasabi's mission is to become the default observability tool in the AI era and enable **self-healing software**. The long-term goal is a world where software improves itself without human intervention, focusing on the maintenance lifecycle which represents a massive, often overlooked opportunity.

## Context

This video is crucial for software engineers, DevOps professionals, and startup founders. Observability is a foundational need for any software company, but traditional tools are complex and manual. The emergence of powerful AI agents creates a paradigm shift, allowing for simpler, more intelligent systems. Founder Sherwood's journey also provides valuable lessons on aligning a startup with personal expertise versus pursuing unfamiliar markets.
